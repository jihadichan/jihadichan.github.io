意大利阿博拉的一个小村庄里住着一个老木匠。他的名字叫杰佩托。他一个人住在一个小屋子里。他没有妻子，也没有孩子。每到夜晚降临，他就会觉得非常寂寞。每天早上，他去森林里搜集雕刻用的木材。
In the small village of Arbora, Italy, there lived an old carpenter. His name was Geppetto. He lived alone in his small house. He had no wife and no children. When it was very late at night, he was often lonely. Every morning, Geppetto went into the forest to collect wood for carving.
“这个树枝和桌腿一样重。但是太短了，我不太满意。” 他说。“这个木材像大碗一样宽，但是不够深，我不太满意。这倒是一块好木材。但是太小了，我什么都做不了。” 
“This branch is heavy like a table leg. But it is too short. I don’t like it,” he said. “This log is wide like a bowl, but it is not deep enough. I don’t like it. This is a good piece of wood. But it is too small. I can’t make anything out of this.”
杰佩托把木头扔到了地上。
“哎呦！” 一个声音说。
“什么？是谁在说话？有人吗？” 杰佩托问。他非常困惑。他转过身，往森林的深处看，但是谁也没看到。
Geppetto threw the wood on the ground.
“Ouch!” said a voice.
“What? Who said that? Is someone there?” Geppetto asked the forest. He was very confused. He turned around and looked deep into the forest, but saw no one.
“一定是我的幻觉。” 他说。于是，他继续往前走。
“喂！不要把我留在这里！我并不小！我是一块儿好木头。我是可以被充分利用起来的！” 那个声音又说。
杰佩托往回看，他吓呆了。那块木头会说话！
“I must be imagining things,” he said, and he kept walking.
“Hey, don’t leave me here! I’m not too small! I am good wood. You can make something out of me!” the voice said again.
Geppetto looked back and was very surprised. It was a talking piece of wood!
“我一定是疯了！” 杰佩托大叫。
“没错，你是疯了！你把一块儿上好的木头丢在森林里。” 
“不对，我觉得自己疯了，因为我在跟一块木头说话！” 
“喂，我可是一块儿非常聪明的木头呢。” 木头反驳道。
“对，但是你只是一块木头！” 
“I must be crazy!” Geppetto cried.
“That’s right, you are crazy! You left a fine piece of wood in the forest.”
“No, I must be crazy because I am talking to a piece of wood!”
“Hey, I am made of very smart pine,” the wood said, offended.
“Yes, but you are only a piece of wood!”
“只是一块儿木头？先生，把我带回家吧。我会让你知道，我不仅仅是一块儿木头而已。” 
杰佩托很担心自己疯了。但是，他也很寂寞。“这个会说话的木头说不定会知道许多有趣的故事和笑话。” 他对自己说。最后，他决定把这块木头带回家。
“Only a piece of wood? Take me home, sir, and I will show you I am not only a piece of wood.”
Geppetto was worried that he was crazy. But, he was also lonely. “This talking piece of wood might know interesting stories or funny jokes,” he said to himself. He decided to take the wood home.
“嗯，我该用你做什么呢？我可以把你雕刻成一根擀面杖，或者把你雕刻成一个鸟舍。” 杰佩托说。
“什么？我可不想被雕刻。听上去太疼了！” 木头说。
“Hmm, what should I do with you? I could carve you into a rolling pin for dough. Or I could carve you into a small birdhouse,” Geppetto said.
“What? I don’t want to be carved. That sounds painful!” said the piece of wood.
“那么如果我不把你雕刻成东西，你永远只能是一块木头。” 
“好吧。你可以把我雕刻成东西。但是，请你一定要把我做成有趣的东西。” 木头说，“我不想做擀面杖。我也不喜欢鸟。我想看看世界，到处去冒险。” 
“Well, if I don’t carve you into something, you will only be a piece of wood.”
“Fine, you can carve me into something. But please, make it something interesting,” said the wood, hopefully. “I don’t want to be a rolling pin. And I don’t like birds. I want to see the world and go on adventures.”
“那做成烟斗怎么样？富有的旅行家可以带着你。” 杰佩托提出他的建议。
“不要，我不喜欢烟。” 
“那我可以把你雕刻成象棋子。这样每天都是游戏！” 杰佩托说。他对自己的想法非常满意。
“How about a pipe? A rich traveler could take you with him,” offered Geppetto.
“No, I don’t like smoke.”
“I could carve you into chess pieces. Every day would be a game!” said Geppetto, proud of this idea.
“不，你根本就不明白。我想跑。我想走。我想说话。一个小男孩儿怎么样？你可以把我做成一个小男孩儿吗？” 
“小男孩儿？嗯……” 杰佩托摸着鼻子想。
“求求你了！我答应你，我一定做一个好男孩。” 木头说。
“No, you don’t understand. I want to run. I want to walk. I want to talk. How about a boy? Can you make me into a boy?”
“A boy? Hmm . . .” Geppetto touched his nose, thinking.
“Yes, please! I promise to be a good boy too,” the wood said.
“如果你把我雕刻成一个小男孩儿，我就是你的儿子。我们可以开开心心地住在一起！” 木头补充说。
其实木头根本就不想和杰佩托一起住。它只想环游世界，到处冒险。木头这么说，只是希望杰佩托能够答应它。
“If you carve me into a boy, that means I will be your son. We can live together and have fun!” it added.
The piece of wood didn’t really want to live with Geppetto. It wanted to travel the world and go on adventures. But, the wood said this hoping Geppetto would agree.
“小男孩儿。好，我觉得这是个好主意。我们马上开始吧！” 杰佩托露出了开心的笑容。
他为自己将有一个儿子感到高兴。他为自己不再寂寞感到高兴。
“A boy. Yes, I think that is a good idea. Let’s get started right away!” said Geppetto, as he smiled a very big smile.
He was happy to have a son. He was happy he was not alone.
杰佩托小心地带着会说话的木头回到了家。杰佩托的家干净整洁。在一个大房间里，有一张床，一张桌子，一些工具和一个火炉。虽然杰佩托努力工作，但他并没有太多钱。然而，杰佩托并不在乎。
Geppetto carried his talking wood home carefully. Geppetto’s home was simple and clean. He had one large room with a bed, a table, his tools, and a stove. Although Geppetto worked hard, he did not have very much money. But he didn’t mind.
“现在我该从哪里开始呢？” 他自言自语地说。“我可从来没有做过小男孩儿呢。” 
“从腿开始吧！这样我就可以跑得很快了！” 木头说。
“不，我觉得你应该先有眼睛，这样你才不会撞到墙。我不希望你受伤。” 杰佩托说。
“Now, where should I start?” he wondered aloud. “I’ve never made a boy before.”
“Start with the legs! That way I can run fast!” said the wood.
“No, I think you had better have eyes first, so you don’t run into walls. I don’t want you to get hurt,” Geppetto said.
杰佩托可以做自己的孩子，他感到非常开心。他先雕刻出脸，接着雕刻出眼睛。当脸逐渐成形，杰佩托开始兴奋地想象自己可以和小男孩儿一起做的事。他们在一起会多么地开心呀！
He was happy to be making his very own boy. He started first by carving out the face, and then he carved the eyes. As the wood looked more like a boy’s face, he became excited about what he and his boy could do together. What fun they would have!
“好了，你的眼睛看上去很灵活。你看得清楚吗？” 杰佩托问。
“天啊，太糟糕了！” 木头大叫。
“怎么了？” 杰佩托担心地说。他以为木头什么也看不见。
“There, your eyes look very smart. How well can you see?” Geppetto asked.
“Oh, it’s terrible!” cried the wood.
“What’s wrong?” asked Geppetto, worried. He thought maybe the wood was blind.
“我们太穷了！看看这个小房间！我想要变富，不是穷。” 木头说。听到小男孩儿为他的家感到羞愧，杰佩托觉得非常伤心。他下定决心，小男孩儿需要的，他一定全都给。
“We are very poor! Look at this small room! I want to be rich, not poor,” the wood said. Geppetto was sad that his boy was ashamed of his home. He promised himself that his boy would have whatever he needed.
“我接下来会雕刻出你的耳朵，这样你就可以听和学习。” 杰佩托说。
杰佩托专心地工作了一整天。他在午饭前雕刻了头发，鼻子和笑脸。然后，他和小男孩儿一起分着吃了第一顿饭，他们吃了面包和奶酪。
“I will carve out your ears next, so that you can hear and learn,” Geppetto said.
Geppetto worked carefully on the wood all through the day. He carved hair, a nose, and a smile before lunch. Then, he shared his first meal of bread and cheese with his boy.
“小男孩儿，我们在一起一定会非常开心。” 杰佩托边吃边说。他撕下一点面包，放进小男孩儿的嘴里。“只是我不应该叫你‘小男孩儿’。你应该有个名字。我应该叫你什么呢？” 
“You and I will have great fun together, boy,” said Geppetto as he ate his meal. He tore off some bread and put it into the boy’s mouth. “Only I shouldn’t call you 'boy'. You need a name. What should we name you?”
“我知道了！你可以叫我大森林里的王子艾德蒙。” 木头说。他喜欢这个名字，因为听上去很大很重要。
“I know! My name can be Prince Edmund of the Great Woods,” said the wood. He liked the name because it sounded big and important.
“对一个小男孩儿来说，这个名字太大了。你是木头做成的，我叫你匹诺曹，怎么样？” 杰佩托说。他想要起一个小男孩儿会喜欢的名字。
“That is too big a name for such a small boy. Since you are made of pinewood, I will call you Pinocchio. What do you think?” asked Geppetto. He wanted to pick a name the boy would like.
“好的。匹诺曹，听上去像是一个很重要的名字。虽然没有大森林里的王子艾德蒙那么好，但是我觉得还不错。” 他答应了。
“太好了！那就定匹诺曹了！为了庆祝，我来雕刻你的手臂和双腿，这样我们就能跳舞了！” 杰佩托快速地雕刻着木头。
“Yes—Pinocchio. That sounds important, though it doesn’t sound as grand as Prince Edmund of the Great Woods. I think it will be fine,” he agreed.
“Wonderful! Pinocchio it is! To celebrate, I will carve your arms and legs so we can dance!” And Geppetto carved quickly.
杰佩托一完成匹诺曹的腿，匹诺曹就跳到地上，从前门跑了出去。
“等等！” 杰佩托喊道，“匹诺曹，你要去哪里？” 
“我要去探险！” 匹诺曹大声回答。
As soon as Geppetto finished carving Pinocchio’s feet, Pinocchio jumped to the floor and ran out of the front door.
“Wait!” Geppetto called out. “Pinocchio, where are you going?”
“I am off to find adventures!” Pinocchio called.
“但是你还没有衣服呢！没穿衣服怎么可以跑走呢！不要走！” 杰佩托一边追着匹诺曹，一边大声喊着。但是，杰佩托忘记把他的刀放在桌子上了。
“But, you don’t have any clothes on yet! You can’t run away if you are naked! Please, don’t leave!” Geppetto cried, as he ran after Pinocchio. Unfortunately, Geppetto forgot to leave his knife on the table.
他手拿着刀，追赶着匹诺曹。邻居们以为杰佩托要伤害匹诺曹，就喊了警察：“警察！警察！快抓住他！” 
He was running after Pinocchio with his knife still in his hand! The neighbors thought he wanted to hurt Pinocchio, so they called for the police: “Police, police! Catch him!”
一个警察就在附近，他跑到街上，刚好抓住了杰佩托。他不给杰佩托解释的机会。警察很怕杰佩托的刀，当天晚上他就把杰佩托关进了监狱。
One of the policemen was nearby, and he ran into the street just in time to catch Geppetto. He would not let Geppetto explain about Pinocchio. The policeman was scared of Geppetto’s knife, so he threw him in jail for the night.
可怜的杰佩托被关进了监狱里。但此时匹诺曹正笑着穿过森林。匹诺曹没有穿衣服，但是他一点儿都不在乎。
“我自由了，我自由了。” 他一边跑一边喊， “我可以跑，可以跳，还可以玩儿！” 
Poor Geppetto was locked in prison. But Pinocchio was laughing and running through the forest. Pinocchio wasn’t wearing clothes, and he didn’t care.
“I’m free, I’m free,” he shouted as he ran. “I can run and leap and play!”
太阳开始落山了，匹诺曹开始觉得累了。于是他回到杰佩托的家里。
“我饿了。” 他说。他找遍了家里所有的食物。但是匹诺曹不知道怎么做饭，所以他没办法吃晚餐。
The sun began to set, and Pinocchio was getting tired. So he returned to Geppetto’s house.
“I’m hungry,” he said, and he searched the house for food. But Pinocchio didn’t know how to cook, so he couldn’t eat.
“我好冷。” 他说，他找遍了家里所有的衣服。但是在杰佩托给他做衣服之前，他逃跑了。他只好把自己裹在毯子里。
“我累了。” 他说。但是杰佩托的床太高了。
“I’m cold,” he said, and he searched the house for clothes. But Pinocchio had run away before Geppetto could make him any clothes. So he wrapped himself in a blanket.
“I’m tired,” he said, but Geppetto’s bed was too tall.
“哇！” 匹诺曹哭道， “我又冷又饿又累，还没有人照顾我！” 大大的泪珠顺着他的木头脸庞滑落。
“如果不是你，杰佩托现在还在这里。” 一个小声音传了过来。
“Whaaa!” Pinocchio cried. “I am cold and hungry and tired, and there is no one here to take care of me!” Big tears ran down his wooden face.
“Well, if you hadn’t been a bad boy, Geppetto would be here,” said a tiny voice.
“谁在那儿？” 匹诺曹害怕地问。
夜幕降临，房间里面越来越黑了。匹诺曹看不到任何人。
“是我。” 那个声音再次传来，并伴随着蟋蟀的叫声。
餐桌上的小蜡烛突然亮了起来。
“Who’s there?” asked Pinocchio, a little scared.
Night was falling and the room was getting dark. Pinocchio couldn’t see anyone.
“I am,” said the voice again, with a chirping sound.
A small candle on the table suddenly lit up.
匹诺曹看到一只蟋蟀从蜡烛上爬了下来。
“哦！是一只虫子！” 匹诺曹说， “你是谁？” 
“我是蟋蟀米克。你可以叫我蟋蟀先生。我住在这个房间里已经有一百年了。但我从来没有见过像你这样的坏孩子！” 他责骂道。
Pinocchio saw a cricket crawling down the candle.
“Oh! You’re a bug!” said Pinocchio. “Who are you?”
“My name is Mick the Cricket. You may call me Mr. Cricket. I have lived in this room for one hundred years. But I have never seen such a bad boy as you!” he scolded.
“我也不喜欢你！” 匹诺曹说。他对蟋蟀先生感到很生气。
“真没礼貌！” 蟋蟀先生叫道。“我一直在想办法帮助你，而杰佩托一直都是一个善良的人。这就是你报答你朋友们的方式吗？
“I don’t like you either!” said Pinocchio. He was mad at the cricket for calling him a bad boy.
“Shame on you for being rude!” chirped the cricket. “I am trying to help you, and Geppetto has been very kind. Is this how you thank your friends?
如果是这样，你不会有什么好朋友的。孩子们应该孝敬父母，但是你没有！” 
匹诺曹用手拍着桌子，他试图把蟋蟀先生压扁。
If so, you will have very bad friends. Children should honor their parents, and you have not!”
Pinocchio banged his hand on the table. He was trying to squash Mr. Cricket!
“我会让你看到！我可以拥有我想要的所有朋友！” 匹诺曹尖叫道， “我可以做我想做的事情！我不用孝敬任何人！我要去冒险。有一天，我会成为又有钱又有名的人！” 
蟋蟀先生跳到地板上，想要逃跑。但是匹诺曹追着他，想要踩扁他。
“I’ll show you! I can have all the friends I want!” Pinocchio screamed. “I can do whatever I want! I don’t have to honor anyone! I am going to go on adventures. One day, I will be rich and famous!”
Mr. Cricket jumped to the floor to run away. But Pinocchio chased him and tried to step on him.
“你的选择非常错误！” 蟋蟀先生说， “总有一天，这些错误的选择会伤害到你。你无法逃避你所做的错误的决定。你必须要学会做一个诚实善良的男孩儿。” 他叫着跑向门口。
“You are making very bad choices!” said the cricket. “One day those bad choices will hurt you. You can’t run away from the choices you’ve made. You must learn to do honest work and study to be a good boy,” he chirped, and he ran toward the door.
“我不会去工作。我也不会去学习。我不会听你说的！我只会做我想做的！” 匹诺曹尖声叫道。
蟋蟀先生跑到了门下面。匹诺曹用力踢了一下门。他走到桌子旁边坐下，哭了起来。
“I won’t work. I won’t study. I won’t listen to what you say! I will do only what I want!” Pinocchio screamed.
Mr. Cricket ran under the door. Pinocchio kicked the door. He walked over to the table, sat down, and started to cry.
“我不会听你的话，也不会听其他任何人的话。” 他哭着说。
慢慢地，他就睡着了。泪水挂在他的鼻子上。
“I won’t listen to you or anyone else,” he cried.
He fell asleep with teardrops dripping off his nose.
第二天早上，警察把杰佩托送回家。这时，匹诺曹正在家里哭。他又累又饿，还没有衣服穿。
The next morning, the police let Geppetto go back to his house. Meanwhile, Pinocchio was at home crying because he was tired and hungry and had no clothes.
当杰佩托回到家时，他很生气。
“你是个坏孩子，匹诺曹。你必须尊重我，听我的话。” 杰佩托说。
“是的，爸爸。对不起。” 匹诺曹说。
When Geppetto came home, he was very angry with Pinocchio for running away.
“You are a bad boy, Pinocchio. You must honor me and obey me,” Geppetto said.
“Yes, Father. I am sorry,” said Pinocchio.
那天早上，他决定尝试做个好孩子。做坏孩子并没有什么意思。他知道，如果他向杰佩托道歉，他就会得到食物。
“我昨晚真的很想你，爸爸。” 匹诺曹说。不过这次是真的。 
Earlier that morning, he had decided to try to be a good boy. Being a bad boy was not much fun. He also knew Geppetto would feed him if he was sorry.
“I missed you last night, Father,” Pinocchio said. It was true, too.
他希望杰佩托可以一直在这里给他食物，让他开心。相反，他昨天又冷又饿，蟋蟀先生还把他弄哭了。
杰佩托听到自己的儿子说想念他，心里非常高兴。 
He had wished Geppetto had been there to feed him and make him happy. Instead, he had been cold and hungry. And that cricket had made him cry.
Geppetto’s heart was happy when he heard his son had missed him.
他并不想生匹诺曹的气。 “可怜的孩子。” 他说， “你看起来很累。你一定又饿又冷。我们吃早餐吧。然后，我给你做衣服穿。” 
He did not want to be angry with Pinocchio. “Poor boy,” he said. “You look tired. You must be hungry with no food, and cold with no clothes. Let’s eat breakfast, and then, we’ll make you something to wear.”
杰佩托从口袋里面拿出三个梨。他是在回家的路上摘的。不过，在他还没来得及细细品尝时，匹诺曹已经把三个梨都吃掉了！
“哦！我还是很饿！” 匹诺曹抱怨道。
“好吧，你不应该浪费食物。把核也啃干净。” 杰佩托说。
Geppetto pulled three pears from his pocket. He had picked them on the way home. But, before he could take a bite, Pinocchio ate all three!
“Oh! I am still hungry!” Pinocchio complained.
“Well, you shouldn’t waste food. Finish the cores,” Geppetto said.
匹诺曹吃掉了核。
“你说得对，爸爸。我觉得饱多了。你早餐吃了什么？” 
“你的微笑。” 杰佩托回答。
匹诺曹这才意识到他把杰佩托的早餐都吃掉了！
Pinocchio ate the cores.
“You’re right, Father. That is much better. What did you eat for breakfast today?”
“Your smile,” Geppetto replied.
Pinocchio realized he had eaten all of Geppetto’s food!
“哦，爸爸！对不起，我吃了你的早餐，而我居然不知道。我真的很自私。我会为你做任何事情。如果你想要我去上学，我也可以。” 
“真的吗？” 杰佩托问。儿子终于开始转变了，杰佩托感到很欣慰。
“Oh, Father! I’m sorry for eating your food. I didn’t think! How selfish of me. I promise to do anything you ask. I’ll even go to school, if you want.”
“Really?” asked Geppetto. He was happy his boy was learning some manners.
“是的，但首先我需要买教科书。我可以要一些钱吗？” 匹诺曹问。

“你想成为一个真正的男孩儿吗？” 她问。
“是的！” 匹诺曹兴奋地说。

“Yes!” said Pinocchio excitedly
“你想救杰佩托吗？” 她问。
“是的！” 匹诺曹说。

“Do you want to save Geppetto?” she asked.
“Oh yes!” said Pinocchio.
“Then listen carefully. To become a real boy, you must obey your father. But first, you must save him. You will save him by honoring him.”
“我不明白。他不在这里我怎么尊重他？” 匹诺曹不解地问。
“你必须尊重他的愿望。杰佩托想让你做什么？” 蓝色小仙女问。
“I don’t understand. How can I honor him if he is not here?” Pinocchio asked, confused.
“You must honor his wishes. What did Geppetto want you to do?” asked the Blue Fairy.
“他想让我上学。” 他说。
“那你必须上学去。你可以和我一起住，每天在村庄里学习。只要你尊重他，你就能救他。” 她说。
匹诺曹听到这些话，很伤心。
“He wanted me to go to school,” he said.
“Then you must go to school. You may live with me and study in the village. Once you honor him, you can save him,” she said.
Pinocchio was sad to hear this.
他不想上学。但是，他想救他的爸爸。于是匹诺曹答应了。匹诺曹变成了学校里最棒的学生。老师对他非常满意。但是，有些学生对匹诺曹很刻薄。

“一个木头脑袋如何能变得聪明呢？” 一个男孩儿问。
“把它做成书架！” 另一个男孩儿说。所有的男孩儿都笑了。
One day after school, some of the boys followed him home.
“How can a wooden head become smart?” one boy asked.
“Use it as a bookend!” the other said. All the boys laughed.
“匹诺曹长大后会变成什么？” 第一个男孩儿问其他人。
“一个小木头男孩儿！” 他们回答。然后他们又笑了。
“我很聪明。总有一天，我会变成一个真正的男孩儿！” 匹诺曹说。
“What will Pinocchio be when he grows up?” the first boy asked the others.
“A little wooden boy!” they answered. And they laughed again.
“I’m smart, and one day, I will be a real boy!” said Pinocchio.
“是吗？好吧，如果你现在就能证明你很聪明，那么今天你就可以变成一个真正的男孩儿。” 一个男孩儿说。
“你这是什么意思？” 匹诺曹问。
“嗯，如果你能做真正的男孩儿做的事情，那你就是一个真正的男孩儿，对吧？” 他问。
“Oh yeah? Well, if you prove you are smart now, you can be a real boy today,” one boy said.
“What do you mean?” asked Pinocchio.
“Well, if you do what real boys do, that would make you a real boy, right?” he asked.
“嗯，应该是。” 匹诺曹说。‘如果我能成为一个真正的男孩儿，我就可以尽快救我的爸爸了。’他心想。
“我能做任何你们能做的事情！” 匹诺曹说，“我会让你们看到我是一个真正的男孩儿。” 
“Well, I guess so,” said Pinocchio. “And if I were a real boy, then I could save Father faster,” he thought to himself.
“I can do anything you can do!” said Pinocchio. “I’ll show you I can be a real boy.”
“是吗？今天，我们要逃到天堂去。如果你是一个真正的男孩儿，你就跟我们来。” 一个男孩儿说。
“天堂是什么？” 匹诺曹问。
“那是一个真正的男孩儿们玩耍的地方。在那里永远不用工作。每顿都有甜点。每一个人都很富有。” 
“Oh yeah? Well, today, we are running away to Paradisle. If you’re a real boy, you’ll come with us,” one boy said.
“What is Paradisle?” Pinocchio asked.
“It’s an island where real boys play all day. They never have to do work. They eat dessert for every meal. And everyone is rich.”
匹诺曹觉得这听起来很不错。所以他跟着男孩儿们走到村庄的尽头。在这里，他们看到了一辆坐满男孩儿的马车。那辆马车被穿着衣服，看起来很不高兴的驴拉着。但是男孩儿们没有注意到他们。
他们跳上了马车，前往小岛的尽头。
This sounded great to Pinocchio. So he followed the boys to the end of the village. There, they met a man with a wagon full of boys. The wagon was pulled by very sad donkeys wearing clothes. But the boys didn’t notice them.
They jumped into the wagon and rode to the end of the island.
“到了，小伙子们！” 马车师傅喊道，“这艘船前往天堂，一个每天都是假期的地方，你们永远都不用工作。在船坐满之前快上船吧！” 
“Here we are, boys!” called the wagon master. “This ferry goes to Paradisle, where every day is a holiday, and you never have to work. Come quickly, before the ferry is full!”
匹诺曹和男孩儿们跑向那艘船。匹诺曹看到很多驴从船上走了下来。但是他并没有觉得奇怪。他想成为一个真正的男孩儿。他想救他的爸爸。在天堂，这两件事他都可以做到，还不用工作！
Pinocchio and the boys ran to the ferry. Pinocchio noticed there were a lot of donkeys coming off the ferry. But he didn’t stop to wonder why. He wanted to be a real boy. And he wanted to save his father. At Paradisle, he could do both and he wouldn’t have to work!
匹诺曹和他的新朋友们在天堂玩得很开心。他们早餐吃了冰淇淋，下午玩儿游戏，晚上想熬到几点就熬到几点。每天都是假期。
Pinocchio and his new friends had lots of fun at Paradisle. They ate ice cream for breakfast, played games all afternoon, and stayed up as late as they wanted. Every day really was a holiday.
他们玩得太开心了，以至于忘记了时间。他们甚至忘记了自己的家和家人。第一次，匹诺曹觉得自己是一个真正的男孩儿。但是他知道自己还是木头做的。
They had so much fun that they forgot how long they had been at Paradisle. They even forgot about their homes and families. For the first time, Pinocchio felt like a real boy. But he knew that he was still made of wood.
所以每天晚上，他都会照照镜子，希望看到变化。一天晚上，当他照镜子的时候，他看到了一点变化。但是不是他想要的变化。他的身上长出了毛！就在这时，有人敲门了。

“匹诺曹，让我进去！” 他的一个朋友喊道。
匹诺曹不想让任何人看到他的毛。
“我现在不在。你待会儿再来吧。” 他说。
“匹诺曹，我知道你在里面，因为你在说话。” 男孩儿说，“现在让我进去——快点！” 
“Pinocchio, let me in!” cried one of his friends.
Pinocchio didn’t want anyone to see his fur.
“Uh, I’m not here now. Come back later,” he said.
“Pinocchio, I know you’re in there because you are talking,” said the boy. “Now let me in—hurry!”
匹诺曹打开门，门前站着他的朋友。他的身上也长出了毛！
“匹诺曹，你必须帮助我！” 那个男孩儿喊道。
“帮你？我不知道怎么帮你！看看我，我也长满了毛！” 匹诺曹说。

“Pinocchio, you have to help me!” the boy cried.
“Help you? I don’t know how to help you! Look at me, I’m furry too!” said Pinocchio.

“孩子们，你们还好吗？” 马车师傅问道。马车师傅是他们的朋友。是他把他们带到天堂来的。他知道该怎么做。

匹诺曹一直跑到海边。蓝色小仙女就站在海边！
“噢，蓝色小仙女，帐篷里的那个人是你吗？” 匹诺曹问。
“是的，匹诺曹。” 她说。
Pinocchio didn’t stop running until he got to the sea. There, by the water, stood the Blue Fairy!
“Oh, Blue Fairy, was that you in the tent?” asked Pinocchio.
“Yes, Pinocchio,” she said.
“哦，我真惭愧。我太傻了，我不是一个好孩子。我很抱歉。你能帮助我吗？”

“But, I don’t want to be a donkey forever. I want to be a boy again. I want to go to school. And I want to save Geppetto,” he cried.
“Perhaps you will do all those things. But first, you must undo your mistakes. How did you become a donkey?” she asked.

“是的，如果你现在开始做完全相反的事，你就会变回木头男孩儿。” 她说完话，就消失了。

匹诺曹想出了一个好主意。
“Yes, and if you do the opposite, you will return to being a wooden boy,” she said, then disappeared.
“The opposite of selfish and lazy is generous and hardworking. How can I be generous and hardworking?” he wondered.
Then, Pinocchio got an idea.
匹诺曹的想法是这样的。他想到了需要帮助的人。他想到了孤独和走失的人。他想到了需要朋友的人。他想到了孤儿。
Pinocchio’s idea was this. He thought of someone who needed help. He thought of someone who was lonely and lost. He thought of someone who needed a friend. He thought of an orphanage.
匹诺曹知道没有家人是什么感觉。很多陌生人曾对他很友善，而且给了他很多东西。他记得蓝色小仙女说过，“从陌生人那里得到帮助是件好事，但是帮助陌生人是件更好的事。” 
Pinocchio knew how it felt not to have a family. Many strangers had been kind to him and had given him a lot. And he remembered the Blue Fairy saying, “It is a great thing to receive help from a stranger, but it is an even greater thing to give help to a stranger.”
“我要去帮助那些没有爸爸妈妈的孩子。” 他决定。
匹诺曹终于找到了通往孤儿院的路。那个地方很小，院子里有很多枯树枝。
“I am going to help children who don’t have a mom or dad,” he decided.
Pinocchio eventually found his way to an orphanage. It was small, and there were many dead trees in the yard.
“我要帮他们移开这些树枝。” 匹诺曹说。
他用绳子把树枝捆起来，然后拉走。他把院子里所有的树枝都清理干净。
孩子们看到了他，都从里面跑了出来。
“哇，一头驴！” 他们喊道。
“I will help move these trees,” Pinocchio said.
He looped a rope around a tree and pulled. He did this for every tree until the yard was clean.
The children saw him in the yard and ran outside.
“Yeah, a donkey!” they cried.
“多么棒的驴啊。” 一个女孩儿说。
“我们能骑你吗，驴先生？” 她问。
匹诺曹点点头。于是女孩儿爬了上去。他带着一个又一个孩子绕着院子走。孩子们开心地笑了。
“What a nice donkey,” one girl said. “Could we ride on your back, Mr. Donkey?” she asked.
Pinocchio nodded, and the girl climbed on. One by one, he gave the children rides around the yard. They laughed and were very happy.
晚饭的时候，孩子们跑进屋里吃通心面条。匹诺曹很饿，他很想进屋。但是他想变得慷慨一些。于是他吃了些草，睡在了外面。
When it was dinnertime, the children ran inside to eat macaroni. Pinocchio was hungry, and he wanted to be inside. But he also wanted to be generous. So he ate grass and slept outside.
每天，匹诺曹都在孤儿院工作。他让孩子们骑着他去城里买食物。他把草吃短点，好让孩子们玩得安全。他搬运柴火。有任何艰难的工作，匹诺曹都很乐意帮忙。
Every day, Pinocchio worked at the orphanage. He gave the children rides into town to buy food. He cropped the grass short so the children could play safely. He carried firewood. If there was any hard job to do, Pinocchio was happy to help.
下午，他会坐在房子外面听孩子们上课。
“孩子们，二加二等于几？” 老师问。
“四！” 他们都喊道。
In the afternoons, he would sit outside the house and listen to the children study their lessons.
“Children, what is two plus two?” the teacher asked.
“Four!” they all shouted.
“孩子们，我们国家的首都是哪个城市？” 老师问。
“罗马！” 他们喊道。
“很好。” 老师说，“现在，到了讲故事的时间了。” 
匹诺曹喜欢听老师讲故事，所以他听得很认真。
“Children, where is our nation’s capital?” the teacher asked.
“Rome!” they all shouted.
“Good,” the teacher said. “Now, it is time for a story.”
Pinocchio loved the teacher’s stories, so he listened carefully.
“从前有一个名叫约拿的男人。他不想听上帝的话，于是逃跑了。但是他被一只鲸鱼吞到了肚子里！他在鲸鱼的肚子里待了三天。由于他非常后悔没有听上帝的话，鲸鱼又把他吐了出来。我们要听上帝的话，孩子们。” 
“Once there was a man named Jonah. He tried to disobey God, and he ran away. But he was swallowed by a whale! He lived in the whale for three days. He was sorry for disobeying God, so the whale spit him onto a beach. We must always obey God, children.”
匹诺曹站了起来。“约拿在鲸鱼肚子里活着？那么爸爸也一定在鲸鱼肚子里活着！蓝色小仙女说过他很安全，还被藏起来了。他一定是在鲸鱼肚子里！噢，如果我待在学校里，我早就能听到这些故事了。我早就可以把爸爸救出来了！” 
Pinocchio sat up. “Jonah lived inside a whale? Of course! Father must be living inside the whale too! The Blue Fairy said he was safe and hidden. That must be where he is! Oh, if only I had stayed in school. I would have learned this story a long time ago. I could have saved Father faster!”
就在这时，匹诺曹发生了巨大的变化。他的蹄子变成了手指，他的耳朵变小了，他的毛脱落了，他的声音听起来又像男孩儿了。
“成功了！” 他喊道，“蓝色小仙女是对的！我既慷慨又勤劳。现在，我又能做回男孩儿了！” 
Just as Pinocchio said this, a big change happened. His hooves changed to fingers. His ears got small again. His fur fell off. And he sounded like a boy again.
“It worked!” he cried. “The Blue Fairy was right! I was generous and worked hard. Now, I can be a boy again!”
匹诺曹以最快的速度跑向大海。这次他再也不会停了，不会因为金子，或者马戏团，或者天堂而停下。他要找到杰佩托！
Pinocchio ran to the ocean as fast as his wooden legs would go. He was not going to stop this time, not for gold or a circus or Paradisle. He was going to find Geppetto!
匹诺曹跑向大海。因为他在孤儿院工作过，所以他很强壮。他顶着海浪轻松地游着。他游得很快，甚至能与海豚一起游泳。
Pinocchio ran to the ocean. Because he had worked at the orphanage, he was very strong. It was easy for him to swim through the waves. And he swam so fast that he was able to swim with some dolphins.
“你好，海豚！” 匹诺曹喊道，“我在寻找一条大鲸鱼。你知道我应该去哪里找吗？” 
“是的，是的。” 海豚们说。
“Hello, dolphins!” Pinocchio called. “I am looking for a large whale. Do you know where I can find it?”
“Yeah, yeah” the dolphins said.
海豚围着匹诺曹游了一个大大的圈。一只海豚游到匹诺曹身下，让他可以游得更深一点。海豚们朝新的方向游去。匹诺曹紧紧地抓着。
“哇，这太有意思了！” 他穿过水里的泡泡。
They swam a big circle around him. One dolphin came below Pinocchio to help him swim deeper. The dolphins swam off in a new direction. Pinocchio held on tightly.
“Whee, this is fun!” he shouted through the bubbles.
海豚们游得越来越深。匹诺曹能看到前面有一块很大的东西。看起来像是一个蓝色的大房子。是鲸鱼！海豚们游向鲸鱼。
“你好，大鲸鱼！” 它们喊道。
“你们好。” 鲸鱼回答。
The dolphins dove deeper and deeper. Pinocchio could see something very big ahead. It looked like a big blue house. It was the whale! The dolphins swam near the whale.
“Hi, big whale!” they called.
“Hello,” the whale called back.
鲸鱼张开嘴说你好的时候，匹诺曹跳进了它的嘴里。
“里面太黑了！” 匹诺曹小心翼翼地沿着鲸鱼的牙齿走着。“但是这里很暖和。” 他深入到鲸鱼的喉咙。
When the whale opened its mouth to say hello, Pinocchio jumped inside.
“It’s so dark in here!” Pinocchio said, walking carefully along the whale’s teeth. “It’s nice and warm though.” He walked deep into the whale’s throat.
当匹诺曹走近鲸鱼的胃的时候，一股难闻的味道传了过来。
“噢，真恶心！” 他说，“是什么闻起来那么臭？” 
“是我。” 一个声音传来。
“爸爸？” 匹诺曹喊道。
As Pinocchio got closer to the whale’s stomach, he noticed a bad smell. “Oh, that’s gross!” he said. "What smells so bad?”
“I do,” said a voice.
“Father?” Pinocchio called.
“匹诺曹，是你吗？” 杰佩托回答。虽然他们身在一个难闻的鱼肚子里，但他们从来没这么开心过。他们向着对方跑去，拥抱彼此。
“Pinocchio, is that you?” Geppetto called back. Even though they were inside a smelly fish, they had never been happier. They ran across the whale’s stomach and held each other.
“噢，爸爸。你瘦了。很抱歉这么长时间才找到你。请原谅我。” 匹诺曹说。
“匹诺曹，你是怎么找到我的？我已经放弃希望了。我以为你也走失了。” 杰佩托说。
“Oh, Father. You are very thin. I am sorry it took me so long to find you. Please forgive me,” Pinocchio said.
“Pinocchio, how did you find me? I had given up hope. I thought you were lost too,” said Geppetto.
他们彼此都有很多问题要问，有很多故事要说。但是鲸鱼在向更深的地方游去。
“爸爸，我们必须马上逃离这条鲸鱼。” 匹诺曹说。
“没用的。” 杰佩托说，“我试过很多次了，但是鲸鱼不肯张开嘴巴。” 
They both had many questions to ask and even more stories to tell. But the whale was swimming deeper.
“Father, we must get out of this whale quickly,” said Pinocchio.
“It is no use,” said Geppetto. “I have tried many times, but the whale will not open its mouth.”
匹诺曹想起约拿的故事。鲸鱼把约拿吐了出来。匹诺曹如何让这条鲸鱼把他们吐出去呢？突然，他想到了办法。
“爸爸，跟我一起爬到鲸鱼的嘴巴上。来，我帮你。” 
Pinocchio remembered the story of Jonah. The whale had spit Jonah out. How could Pinocchio make this whale spit them out? Suddenly, he had an idea.
“Father, climb with me up to the whale’s mouth. Here, I will help you.”
他们一起爬了上去，坐在鲸鱼的牙齿旁。
“你能站起来挠鲸鱼上口腔的痒吗？” 匹诺曹说。
“我可以试试。” 杰佩托回答。
Together, the two climbed up and sat on the whale’s teeth.
“Can you reach up and tickle the top of the whale’s mouth?” Pinocchio asked.
“I will try,” answered Geppetto.
他站起来，用手挠鲸鱼的痒。鲸鱼开始快速地向上游去。鲸鱼的舌头开始颤抖。
“阿嚏！” 鲸鱼打了个喷嚏。就这样，它把父子俩吐到了沙滩上。
“耶，我们成功了！” 匹诺曹欢呼。
He stood up tall and tickled the whale. The whale started to swim up very quickly. Its tongue started to jump.
“Ahh­-ahh-­choo!”the whale said, and it sneezed them onto the sand.
“Hooray, we made it!” cheered Pinocchio.
“噢，你太聪明了，儿子！” 杰佩托说。
“你怎么知道去挠鲸鱼的痒？” 
“我从学校里学到的知识中获得了灵感。” 匹诺曹笑着说。
“Oh, you are so smart, son!” said Geppetto.
“How did you know to tickle the whale?”
“I got the idea from something I learned at a school," Pinocchio said, smiling.
就在这时，那只鲸鱼滑到沙滩上开始说话。它的声音又温柔又好听。
“做得好，匹诺曹。” 它说，“你已经证明了自己是个好孩子。你尊重你的爸爸。你很听话，也很慷慨。你冒着生命危险救了他。” 
Just then, the whale began to slide up onto the sand and speak. Its voice was soft and pretty.
“Well done, Pinocchio,” it said. “You have proven yourself to be a good boy. You have honored your father. You have been obedient and generous. You have risked your life to save him.”
突然，那只鲸鱼消失了。蓝色小仙女出现在了那里。
“蓝色小仙女！你就是那条鲸鱼？” 匹诺曹惊讶地问。
“是的。我保护着杰佩托，直到你学会做一个好孩子。再见，朋友们。祝你们幸福。” 说完，蓝色小仙女飞走了。
Suddenly, the whale vanished. In its place stood the Blue Fairy.
“Blue Fairy! You were the whale?" Pinocchio asked, very surprised.
“Yes. I kept Geppetto safe until you learned to be a good boy. Farewell, gentlemen. I wish you a happy life together,” she said, and the Blue Fairy flew away.
匹诺曹和杰佩托挥着手告别。匹诺曹挥手时看到了自己的手。它变成了一只真正男孩儿的手。
“啊！我是一个真正的男孩儿了！爸爸，看！我是真的！” 他喊道。
爸爸和儿子笑了，他们拥抱后，跳着舞，高高兴兴地回了家。
Pinocchio and Geppetto waved good-bye. As Pinocchio waved, he looked at his hand. It was the hand of a real boy.
“Ah! I’m a real boy! Father, look! I’m really real!” he cried.
Father and Son laughed and hugged, and they danced all the way home.
