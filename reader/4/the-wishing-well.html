搬一次家可真不容易。从芝加哥这样的大城市搬到一个小山村就更不容易了。我们来问问巴斯家的孩子们吧。
“这里没什么好玩的！” 名叫赛伊的老大抱怨道。
Moving is never easy. Moving from a big city like Chicago to a small town is even harder. Just ask the Barth children.
"There's nothing to do here!" complained the oldest, named Cy.
“这里什么商店都没有。” 老二安西娅也在抱怨。她的兄弟姐妹亲切地叫她潘瑟。那时，她的弟弟鲍比年纪很小。他还不会叫安西娅，所以他就叫她潘瑟。现在其他几个兄弟姐妹也这么叫她了。
"I don't see any shops," whined Anthea, the second eldest. Among her siblings, her nickname was Panther. When her younger brother, Bobby, was little, he couldn't say Anthea. He called her Panther instead, and now the others did too.
“您这是什么意思？我们不能上网？” 鲍比哭着说。
“咦！那是什么？” 简尖叫道。一只棕色的小田鼠从台阶下迅速跑出来，冲进了草从里。
"What do you mean, we won't have Internet?" cried Bobby, looking up at his father.
"Eeek! What's that?" Jane screamed. A little brown field mouse scurried from under the steps and into the grass.
没有一个人想要搬到玛莎阿姨的家里，但爸爸丢掉了工作，他们没有其他的选择了。
孩子们坐在餐桌边妈妈尽力安慰他们说：“我们就在这里小住几天。” 
“这里有很多事情可以做。” 玛莎阿姨说。
No one in the family had wanted to move to Aunt Martha's house. But Father had lost his job, and they had no choice. "It's only for a short while," Mother said, trying to comfort the children as they sat at the kitchen table.
"And there're lots of things to do around here," said Aunt Martha.
“对啊，比如抓蚱蜢?” 赛伊没好气的说。
“蚱蜢？哪里？” 简尖叫道。
更糟糕的是，妈妈和爸爸第二天一早就离开了。爸爸要去芝加哥参加几个工作面试。
"Yeah, like catch grasshoppers?" Cy said in a rather nasty way.
"Grasshoppers? Where?" screamed Jane.
To make matters worse, Mother and Father left the next morning. Father had some job interviews in Chicago.
孩子们孤孤单单地站在台阶上，看着小汽车慢慢开走了。
“咩，咩。” 小婴儿开心地大叫。
“哦，兰姆。没什么事情能让你觉得伤心，对不对？” 潘瑟笑着说。小婴儿希拉里会有这个昵称是因为它说的第一个词是“咩” ，就像小羊羔一样。
The children stood alone on the steps as the car pulled away.
"Baa, baa," cried the baby happily.
"Oh, Lamb. Nothing makes you sad, does it?" Panther laughed. Hilary, the baby, got his nickname because the first word he said was "baa," just like a little lamb.
孩子们不想回到房子里。那里什么也没得玩儿。玛莎阿姨家后面是一片茂密的森林。因为没人有更好的主意了，于是他们就出发去森林里探险了。
“看！” 鲍比激动地指着一大群浣熊。
The children didn't want to go back inside the house. There was nothing to do there. Behind Aunt Martha's house was a thick forest. And since no one had a better idea, they set off to explore the woods.
"Look!" Bobby pointed excitedly at a family of raccoons.
“闻闻！” 潘瑟一边说一边弯下腰去闻这些美丽的花朵。
一整天，孩子们都在森林里搜索新的发现。过了一会儿，他们发现自己身在茂密的丛林中。
"Smell these!" Panther said, as she bent over some colorful flowers.
All day, the children searched the forest for new discoveries. After a while, they found themselves in a thicket of trees.
“这里黑的好可怕。” 潘瑟有点儿担心地说。
“或者我们应该回去了。” 简补充道。
“嘿！看这个！” 赛伊激动地大叫。赛伊发现了一口古老的枯井。
"It's awfully dark in here," Panther said in a worried voice.
"Maybe we should go back," Jane added.
"Hey! Look at this!" Cy called out excitedly. Cy had found an old, dried-up well.
“你能看到什么东西吗？” 简问。所有人都弯腰向井里看去。
“没有！但是我感觉这里面会很有趣。” 赛伊回答说。
“里面有水吗？” 鲍比问道。
孩子们盯着井里更深的地方，他们的眼睛越睁越大。
"Can you see anything?" asked Jane, as they all peered down the well.
"Nope! But there's a funny smell," replied Cy.
"Is there any water?" Bobby asked.
The children stared deeper into the well, their eyes getting wider.
“我在想这会不会是一个许愿井。” 简说。
“等等！看这里。” 潘瑟说。潘瑟在枯井的旁边发现了一个破旧的木桶系在绳子上。“我们把这个桶放到井里去，看看那下面有什么吧。
” 她说。“她在干嘛？” 鲍比指着简说。
"I wonder if this is a wishing well," said Jane.
"Wait! Look here," said Panther. Next to the well, Panther had found an old wooden bucket attached to some rope. "Let's lower this bucket and see what's down there," she said.
"What's she doing?" Bobby said, pointing to Jane.
简闭上了眼睛，拿着一枚硬币。“我在许愿。” 她回答说。她转身将硬币越过肩膀扔进了井里。叮当！叮当！叮当！
“哎呦！” 井里传出一声大叫。
“你们听见了吗？” 鲍比小声说。
Jane's eyes were closed, and she was holding onto a coin. "I'm making a wish," she replied. She turned around and threw the coin over her shoulder and into the well. Clink, clink, clonk.
"Ouch!" a voice cried from the well.
"Did you hear that?" whispered Bobby.
“你好，下面的！” 赛伊大声叫道。“你好，下面的！” 他的声音在井里回荡着。然后一切都安静了。
“我肯定我听到了的声音。” 鲍比说。
“你觉得那是动物吗？” 简担心地问。
“要找到答案就只有一个方法。” 赛伊激动地说。他将桶向下放到了井里。
"Hello, down there!" Cy shouted. "Hello, down there!" his voice echoed inside the well. Then silence.
"I'm sure I heard something," said Bobby.
"Do you think it's an animal?" Jane asked worriedly.
"There's only one way to find out," Cy said excitedly. He lowered the bucket down the well.
“你能感觉到什么吗？” 潘瑟问。
“快点，你能感觉到什么吗？” 鲍比耐不住性子地说。
赛伊将桶拉了回来。桶里满是泥巴、汽水罐、旧鞋子，还有一些石头。赛伊又将木桶放到井底好几次。如果井里有什么东西，他下定决心一定要把它找出来。
"Can you feel anything?" Panther asked.
"Hurry up, will you?" Bobby said impatiently.
Cy pulled the bucket back up. It was full of mud, soda cans, an old shoe, and some rocks. Cy lowered the bucket again several times. If there was something in the well, he was determined to find it.
一次又一次地把木桶放下去，赛伊觉得有些烦了。其他人也想要放弃了。赛伊深深地吐了一口气。“就这样吧。井里没什么特别的东西。” 他一边说一边将最后一桶东西倒出来。
After a while, Cy got tired of lowering the bucket. The others wanted to give up too. Cy let out a deep breath. "That's it. There's nothing else down there," he said, emptying a final bucket load.
“等等。” 潘瑟叫道，“看这个！” 她捡起了一块外形奇特的贝壳儿，上面有很多泥浆。
“这是什么？” 简问。她凑近了去看那个东西。
潘瑟抹去了一些泥浆，把它拿在手里。它很美。“我觉得这是个贝壳。” 她回答说。
"Wait," cried Panther. "Look at this!" She picked out an unusual shell, covered in mud.
"What is it?" Jane asked, looking closer.
Panther wiped off some of the mud and held it up. It was beautiful. "I think it's a seashell," she replied.
她开始抹去余下的泥浆。突然，那个贝壳开始咯咯笑。潘瑟吓了一大跳，差点儿扔了它。
“它笑了！” 鲍比大叫道。
“里面有东西。” 他在壳状物上敲了敲。
“噢！别这样！” 壳状物叫道。
She began wiping off the rest of the mud. Suddenly, the shell began to giggle. Panther was so shocked, she almost dropped it.
"It laughed!" Bobby shouted loudly. "Something's inside." He knocked on the shell.
"Ouch! Stop that!" the shell cried.
孩子们都你看看我我看看你。然后，最奇妙的事情发生了。一个生物从壳子里探出头来。它有着海马的脸。它的脖子和背上有不断开合的闪闪发光的鳍。当它的脑袋左右转动时，那对儿鳍也闪闪发光。
The children all looked at each other. Then the most amazing thing happened. A creature poked its head out from the shell. It had the face of a seahorse. On its neck and back were shimmery fins that opened and closed. As its head circled left and right, the fins glowed and shone.
孩子们第一次遇到这种奇事，都愣住了。
“好了？” 那个生物生气地问道。
最后，潘瑟开口了：“你是什么？” 
那个生物盯着他们。“那我就告诉你们。” 它开始说。
For the first time in their lives, the children were speechless.
"Well?" the creature asked in an irritated voice.
Finally, Panther spoke up. "What are you?"
The creature gazed at them. "Let me get this straight," it began.
“你们朝我扔东西，把我吵醒了。” 它盯着简。“是你一开始拿走我的东西。” 它看着赛伊。“你挠我痒痒。” 它瞪着潘瑟。“然后，你在我的壳上敲来敲去！” 鲍比低头看着地面。“现在，你们告诉我说你们连我是谁都不知道？” 
"You throw things on me and wake me up." It glared at Jane. "You start taking away my things." It looked at Cy. "You tickle me." It stared at Panther. "Then you bang on my shell!" Bobby looked down at the ground. "And now, you tell me that you don't even know what I am?"
孩子们尴尬地盯着地面。“真对不起！” 简抱歉地说。“我们不知道你在井里。” 
那个生物什么都没说。过了一会，它的鳍不再闪光了。“我接受你们的道歉。” 最后它说。
The children stared at the ground in embarrassment. "We're sorry," apologized Jane. "We didn't know you were in the well."
The creature didn't say anything. After a while, its fins stopped glowing. "Apology accepted," it said finally.
“但我们真的不知道你是谁。” 赛伊迅速补充说。
“我是奥伦八世，” 它郑重地说，“我是我们种族在整个海洋里的第八代。” 
“哇。” 孩子们齐声叫出来。
“不过你们可以叫我休伊。” 那个生物笑了。
"But we really don't know what you are," Cy added quickly.
"I am Huit des Orlanes," it announced. "The eighth of my kind of the sea."
"Wow," the children said in unison.
"But you can call me Huey," the creature smiled.
孩子们的脸上还是一片茫然，显然他们还是不知道他到底是谁。“你们知道什么是奥伦吗？” 他问。
“实际上不知道。” 简替所有人回答了。
休伊猛地闭上了眼睛。他的脑袋快速地左右转动着。
It was clear from the children's blank faces that they still did not know what he was. "Do you know what an Orlane is?" he asked.
"Not really," Jane admitted for them all.
Huey closed his eyes dramatically. His head turned quickly from side to side.
“从前，” 他说，“我们奥伦是海洋里最有名的神秘生物。” 
“你是说，你是个妖精？” 鲍比打断了他的话。
“妖精？噢，我的天，哦，我的天哪！” 休伊的鳍又开始一边发光一边不断快速地扇动着。
"At one time," he said, "we, Orlanes, were the best-known mystic creatures of the sea."
"You mean, you're a fairy?" interrupted Bobby.
"A fairy? Oh, my, oh, my!" Huey's fins started to glow again as they opened and closed quickly.
“请不要生气，休伊。” 简说，“鲍比的意思并不是要把你叫成妖精。” 
“那好吧。” 休伊平静地说。“奥伦拥有天赐的能力，可以让其他人实现任何愿望。” 他继续说。
“你是说，你可以让愿望成真？” 鲍比问。
"Please don't be upset, Huey," said Jane. "Bobby didn't mean to call you a fairy."
"All right then," Huey said calmly. "Orlanes have the gift to grant others whatever they want," he continued.
"You mean you can make wishes come true?" Bobby asked.
休伊点点头，然后其他孩子就和鲍比一起开心地大叫起来。
“但是，” 休伊提高声音道，“你们必须保证两件事。” 
“只要能实现愿望，我们任何事情都愿意做！” 简拍手道。
Huey nodded, and the other children joined Bobby in shouts of delight.
"However," Huey said in a loud voice. "There are two things you must promise."
"We'll do anything to get our wish!" Jane clapped her hands.
休伊继续说下去。“首先，” 他说，“你们一天只可以许一个愿望。第二，在许下愿望之后，你们必须将我送回井里。” 
“这听起来并不是很难。” 赛伊笑着说。
Huey continued. "First," he said, "you can only make one wish a day. And second, after making your wish, you must return me to the well."
"That doesn't sound too hard." Cy laughed.
“还有，” 休伊笑了，“日落的时候，一切都会恢复到原来的样子。” 
“你是说，我们的愿望只能实现一天？” 鲍比用失望的语气问。
“是的！” 休伊回答之后就消失在了他的壳里。
"By the way," Huey smiled, "at sunset, everything returns to the way it was."
"You mean, our wish is true only for one day?" asked Bobby in a disappointed voice.
"Yes," Huey replied, and with that, he disappeared back into his shell.
第二天早上，孩子们吃早饭的速度特别快。一想到能再次见到休伊，大家都很兴奋。
“哪儿着火了？” 玛莎阿姨问。
赛伊给大家使了个眼色。
The next morning, the children could hardly eat their breakfast fast enough. They were excited about visiting Huey again.
"Where's the fire?" Aunt Martha asked.
Cy gave his siblings a warning look.
他们都保证过不会向任何人提起休伊。“我们今天只是去树林里转转。” 鲍比说。他的嘴里塞满了煎饼。
前一天，赛伊按照休伊的要求将它送回了井底。
They had promised not to speak about Huey to anyone. "We're just going out to the woods today," Bobby said, his mouth full of pancake.
The previous day, Cy had lowered Huey back down the well, just as Huey had asked.
鲍比非常聪明，他在通往那口井的小路上做了标记。但他们找那条小路找了很久，却还是在原地打转。
“你确定是这条路吗，鲍比？” 简嘀咕道。
“你用什么做标记的？” 潘瑟问。 
And Bobby had cleverly marked the trail that led to the well. But after searching for the trail for a while, they seemed to be going in circles.
"Are you sure it's this way, Bobby?" Jane whined.
"What did you use to mark the trail?" asked Panther.
“花生啊。” 鲍比回答。遗憾的是，鲍比用来在小路上做标记的那些花生都被浣熊一家子吃光了。
“很好！我们永远也找不到小路了！” 赛伊大叫。
孩子们迷茫地看看四周。
"Peanuts," replied Bobby. Unfortunately for the children, a family of raccoons had eaten Bobby's trail markers.
"Just great! We'll never find the trail!" cried Cy.
The children looked around in confusion.
“走这条路试试吧！” 潘瑟说。他们向她说的方向走去。几个小时过去了，但他们依然连那口井的影子都没看到。
“我们迷路了。我们现在永远都不会找到休伊了。” 简抱怨说。
"Let's try this path," Panther said. They headed off in the direction she pointed. Hours passed, but there was still no sign of the well.
"We're lost. We'll never find Huey now," complained Jane.
“等等！那边是什么？” 赛伊指着某个地方，他激动地跳了起来。“看！我们找到那口井了！” 
孩子们跑向了那口井。赛伊迅速将木桶放到了井底并把那个贝壳拉了上来。
“休伊！休伊！” 他们一起叫道。
"Wait! What's that over there?" cried Cy, pointing and jumping up and down. "Look! We found the well!"
The children raced to the well. Cy quickly lowered the bucket and pulled up the shell. "Huey, Huey," they called together.
休伊慢慢从他的壳里探出了脑袋。
“哦，又是你们！” 他迷迷糊糊地说，“我还在希望遇见你们不过是个糟糕的梦而已。” 
潘瑟决定友好地问一些事情：“休伊，你怎么会在这个井里呢？” 
Huey's head slowly peeked out of his shell. "Oh, it's you again," he said sleepily. "I was hoping that meeting you had been just a bad dream."
Panther decided to ask something friendly: "Huey, how did you end up in this well?"
“这对你们来说可能就是一口老井，” 休伊说，“但这口井对我们这样的海洋生物来说却是一个旅行站。” 
“你要去哪里？” 简问。
"It may look like just an old well to you," Huey said. "But this well is also a traveling station for sea creatures like me."
"Where are you going?" asked Jane.
“我要去马维斯提见我的兄弟们。这口井不过是个临时停留的地方。” 
“你的意思是，你要离开？” 鲍比哀叹道，“什么时候？” 
“时间到了的时候。” 休伊肯定地回答，“现在，你们想好了愿望没有？” 
"I am journeying to Marvesti to meet my brothers. This well is only a temporary stop."
"You mean, you're going to leave?" Bobby wailed. "When?"
"When the time is right," Huey replied in a firm voice. "Now, do you have a wish or not?"
所有孩子都非常激动，但却都没想好要许什么样的愿。他们互相看看对方。“我可没时间一天到晚的这样等着，你们也一样。” 休伊说。
“我觉得赛伊应该第一个许愿。” 简说。
In all their excitement, the children hadn't thought about what to wish for. They looked at each other. "I haven't got all day and neither do you," Huey pointed out.
"I think Cy should make the first wish," said Jane.
“谢谢你，简。” 赛伊沾沾自喜地说。
“为什么是赛伊第一个？” 鲍比抱怨说。
他们三个人争论的时候，潘瑟小声跟自己说：“我希望我们都美丽英俊。” 
"Thank you, Jane," said Cy smugly.
"Why should Cy go first?" complained Bobby.
While the three of them argued, Panther said softly to herself, "I wish we were beautiful and handsome."
突然，休伊的鳍发出了粉红和红色的光芒。它们优雅地开合了三次。孩子们停止了争论，全都惊奇地看着休伊。然后他们看到一团白雾。
Suddenly Huey's fins glowed pink and red. They opened and closed gracefully three times. The children stopped arguing and looked at Huey in amazement. Then each of them felt a light, soft mist on his or her face.
“发生了什么？” 鲍比害怕地问。
“你的愿望正在实现。” 休伊回答。
“我们的愿望？什么愿望？” 赛伊疑惑地问。
他们感觉那些雾离开了自己的脸。
“变得美丽英俊的愿望啊。” 休伊说。
"What's happening?" Bobby asked in a scared voice.
"Your wish is being granted," replied Huey.
"Our wish? What wish?" Cy asked, puzzled.
They felt the mist lift from their faces.
"Your wish to become beautiful and handsome," said Huey.
赛伊、鲍比和简互相看着，又看看潘瑟。他们都在想休伊说的是什么意思，但孩子们在盯着对方的时候互相都不认识了。就像潘瑟许的愿一样，他们每个人都变得像电影明星一样美丽英俊了。
Cy, Bobby, and Jane looked at each other, and at Panther too, wondering what Huey meant. But none of the children recognized the faces staring back. Just as Panther had wished, each of them was as beautiful or handsome as a movie star.
巴斯家的孩子们都认不出对方了。“赛伊？” 潘瑟一边问一边去碰一个小男孩儿。他有着黑色的卷发和清澈的蓝眼睛。
“潘瑟？” 赛伊说。他摸到的是她挽成一个扣的金色长卷发。潘瑟的头发之前通常是棕色直发，发型也非常简单。
The Barth children could not recognize each other. "Cy?" Panther asked, reaching out to a young boy with dark, wavy hair and clear blue eyes.
"Panther?" Cy said. He touched a long wavy lock of her golden hair. Panther's hair was normally straight brown and quite plain.
“谁有镜子？” 简问。“我看上去怎么样？” 简也漂亮极了。她的短发是红色的，微微卷着坠在脸旁。她的皮肤像玫瑰一样娇嫩。她还有了一双像绿宝石一样的眼睛。
"Does anyone have a mirror?" asked Jane. "What do I look like?" Jane was absolutely beautiful. She had short red hair with little curls around her face. Her skin was rosy, and she had bright, shining green eyes.
“我不知道为什么每个人都想变漂亮。” 鲍比抱怨说。“这是你的主意，对不对，潘瑟？” 
“对，我许了愿，” 潘瑟承认了，“但我很高兴可以美丽一次。” 
"I don't know why anyone wants to be beautiful," complained Bobby, "This was your idea, wasn't it, Panther?"
"Yes, I wished it," admitted Panther, "but I'm glad to be beautiful for once."
“很好，很漂亮，你们都是。” 休伊打断了他们。“现在，你们走吧。记得，你们的愿望日落时就会消失。” 休伊缩回了他的壳里。赛伊小心地将他送回了井底。

鲍比无助地看着清单。“但是......” 他开口说。
然而，玛莎阿姨已经消失在了过道里。
"I'm going to get the vegetables," Aunt Martha said when they got to Mr. Keefer's. "Here is the list. You get the other things and I'll meet you at the cash register."
Bobby looked helplessly at the list. "But . . . ," he began.
But Aunt Martha had already disappeared down an aisle.
“无糖玉米罐头。” 鲍比念着清单。他开始在过道里上下寻找，但却始终找不到该往哪里走。“要是潘瑟在就好了。” 他低声说。“她一定知道这些东西都摆在哪里。” 
"Unsweetened canned corn," read Bobby. He searched up and down the aisle but could not figure out where to go. "If only Panther were here," he said under his breath. "She would know where everything is."
鲍比只找到了玛莎阿姨购物清单上的一样东西：番茄汤。他拿着它去了结账柜台。基弗先生隔着厚厚的眼镜片儿盯着他。鲍比试着微笑，但他是一个害羞的男孩儿，他觉得很不自在。
Bobby could find only one item on Aunt Martha's list: tomato soup. He took it to the counter. Mr. Keefer peered at him from behind thick glasses. Bobby tried to smile, but being a shy boy, he felt very uncomfortable.
简是这几个孩子里面最外向的。她总是微笑着和每个人交谈。鲍比注意到了结账柜台上一只泰迪熊身上系着的彩色气球。“一个气球多少钱？” 他问。兰姆特别喜欢玩儿气球，所以鲍比想给他买一个。
Jane was the most outgoing of the children. She smiled and talked to everyone.
Bobby noticed colorful balloons tied to a teddy bear on the counter. "How much for a balloon?" he asked. Lamb loved to play with balloons, so Bobby wanted to get him some.
“二十五美分。” 基弗先生回答。
“我要两个。” 鲍比一边说一边数钱。
“你不觉得这个对你来说有点幼稚了吗？” 玛莎阿姨在鲍比身后说。
“不是给我的。” 鲍比咯咯笑，“给兰姆的。” 
"Twenty-five cents," replied Mr. Keefer.
"I'll take two," Bobby said as he counted out the money.
"Aren't you a little old for that?" Aunt Martha said from behind.
"Not for me," giggled Bobby, "for Lamb."
玛莎阿姨奇怪地看了看鲍比。
“谁？” 她问。
“给小婴……” 鲍比开口时，还没意识到兰姆已经不存在了。“嗯，没事。我不想买了。” 他迅速说道。不知道为什么，鲍比突然有点儿想哭。
Aunt Martha gave Bobby a strange look. "Who?" she asked.
"For the bab . . . ," Bobby began before he realized that there was no Lamb. "Uh, nothing. Maybe I won't take them," he said quickly. For some reason, Bobby suddenly felt like crying.
“你不想要个糖吗？” 基弗先生递来了一个装满彩色糖果的罐子。
鲍比谨慎地选了一个。
“继续拿吧。” 基弗先生说，“多拿一点儿。” 
以前鲍比不得不和他的兄弟姐妹分享一切。
"Do you want a candy?" Mr. Keefer held out a jar full of colored sweets.
Bobby carefully took one.
"Go on," Mr. Keefer said, "take more."
Usually Bobby had to share everything with his brother and sisters.
现在他是一个人了，所以他可以享受更多的一切。鲍比开心地抓了满满一把糖果。
玛莎阿姨因为鲍比没有在购物中帮更多的忙而感到生气。但是，她还是给他做了最喜欢的三明治之后才出门去见她的朋友。
Since he was alone now, he could have more of everything. Happily, Bobby grabbed a handful of candies.
Aunt Martha was upset that Bobby had not helped more with the shopping. Still, she made him his favorite sandwich before leaving to meet her friend.
虽然鲍比很喜欢花生黄油配腌黄瓜三明治，但一个人吃，一点儿意思都没有。
周围安静的让他感到悲伤，所以他出门了。他捡起一个足球，但没人和他一起玩儿。
Even though Bobby loved peanut butter and pickle sandwiches, it was no fun to eat this snack alone.
The silence around him was making him sad, so he went outside. He picked up a soccer ball, but there was nobody to play with.
他骑上自行车，但一个人骑车也没意思。他抬头看看阳光灿烂的天空。“还有多久太阳才会落山啊？” 他想。这一天似乎比以往任何时候都要长。
鲍比努力地去想他的兄弟姐妹们曾对他做过的一切坏事。
He got on his bike, but it was no fun riding by himself. He looked up at the bright sunny sky. "How long before the sun sets?" he wondered. The day seemed to be unusually long.
Bobby thought very hard of all the bad things his brother and sisters did to him.
他努力装作一点儿也不思念他的兄弟姐妹的样子。“赛伊总是戏弄我。” 他大声说。“简总是尖叫。潘瑟一直都喜欢发号施令。兰姆一天到晚地哭。” 但事实上，他非常想念他们。
突然，一个可怕的想法闯进了他的脑袋。
He tried to act as if he didn't miss his siblings. "Cy always teases me," he said aloud. "Jane always screams. Panther acts like she's the boss. Lamb cries all the time." But the truth was, he missed them terribly.
Then a horrible thought entered his head.
要是他再也见不到赛伊、潘瑟、简和兰姆了怎么办？鲍比的心开始砰砰跳。“如果休伊走了，我的愿望永远不会消失了怎么办？” 他说。他怕极了，怕得说不出话来。鲍比用最快的速度穿过了森林。
What if he never saw Cy or Panther or Jane or Lamb again? Bobby's heart began to thump loudly. "What if Huey is gone and my wish will never be undone?" he said. Too scared to say anything more, Bobby ran as fast as he could through the woods.
他跑到了休伊的井边，然后试着将那个木桶从井底拉上来。
“休伊！休伊！我改变主意了！” 鲍比大叫着用尽全力去拉那个木桶。不知为什么，那个木桶在井底一动不动。
He reached Huey's well and tried to pull the bucket up.
"Huey! Huey! I've changed my mind!" Bobby cried, pulling with all his might. For some reason, the bucket would not move.
“不！不！” 鲍比大叫着，但绳子依然纹丝不动，木桶静静地停留在井底。鲍比跌坐在地上大哭。他怕得要死，又觉得非常孤单。最后，他精疲力尽地闭上了眼睛。
"No! No!" screamed Bobby, but the rope remained taut, and the bucket stuck firmly to the bottom of the well. Bobby fell to the ground, crying, afraid, and very much alone. Exhausted and tired, he closed his eyes.
鲍比再次睁开眼睛的时候，他过了好一会儿才想起自己在哪里。他一定是在休伊的井边睡着了。
“鲍比？” 远处传来了一个熟悉的声音。
When Bobby opened his eyes again, it took a moment for him to remember where he was. He must have fallen asleep next to Huey's well.
"Bobby?" a familiar voice called from the distance.
鲍比的心怦怦直跳。他知道那个声音。赛伊、潘瑟和简从树后面向他跑来。鲍比忍不住哭了。“我真的非常非常抱歉。” 他大哭着去拥抱他的兄弟姐妹。
Bobby's heart skipped a beat. He knew that voice. From the trees, Cy, Panther, and Jane came running toward him. Bobby could not hold back his tears. "I'm so sorry," he cried as he hugged his brother and sisters.
他们奇怪地看着他，但却一句话都没说。
太阳落山了。鲍比再次见到家人后，实在是太开心了，几乎压抑不住自己的快乐。
They looked at him strangely, but did not say anything.
As the sun disappeared behind the hill, Bobby was so glad to see his family again that he could have burst with happiness.
晚餐的时候，孩子们围坐在餐桌边吃饭。鲍比因为和自己的兄弟姐妹再一次在一起吃饭而感到非常开心。“你们怎么知道我在井边的？” 他问他们。
“玛莎阿姨担心了。” 潘瑟回答，“我们一整天都没看到你。” 
At dinnertime, the children sat around the table to eat. Bobby felt happy to be eating a meal again with his siblings. "How did you know I was at the well?" he asked them.
"Aunt Martha was getting worried," Panther answered. "We hadn't seen you all day."
“所以我们找不到你的时候，我们就决定去森林里看看。” 赛伊最后说。
“够了，简。” 潘瑟批评道。简拿了很多炸鸡，她根本吃不了那么多。“把这些留给鲍比。” 潘瑟从简那里把盘子端走了。
"So when we couldn't find you, we decided to look in the woods," finished Cy.
"That's enough, Jane," scolded Panther. Jane was taking more fried chicken than she could eat. "Leave those for Bobby." Panther took the plate away from her.
“没关系的。简能吃完的。” 鲍比微笑着说。
大家吃惊地地盯着他。
“你今天实在太奇怪了。你还好吗？” 赛伊用关心的语气问。
鲍比点点头，认真地问：“你们今天都做了什么？” 
"That's okay. Jane can have them all," smiled Bobby.
The others stared at him in shock.
"You've been acting strange. Are you okay?" Cy asked in a concerned voice.
Bobby nodded and carefully asked, "What did you do today?"
赛伊打了个哈欠回答说：“今天真的很奇怪。我感觉自己一整天都在睡觉。” 
“我也是。” 潘瑟说，“我都不记得自己做了什么。” 
“我什么梦都没做。” 简说。她塞了一嘴的炸鸡。
Cy yawned and replied, "It was really weird. I feel like I've been sleeping all day."
"Me, too," said Panther. "And I don't remember doing anything."
"I didn't have any dreams," Jane said, her mouth full of chicken.
“玛莎阿姨说她以为我们去基弗先生店里了，但她不确定。” 潘瑟说。
“等一下。” 赛伊怀疑地看着鲍比，“你见过休伊了吗？你许了什么愿望？” 
"Aunt Martha says she thinks we went to Mr. Keefer's, but she's not sure," said Panther.
"Wait a minute." Cy looked at Bobby suspiciously. "Did you see Huey? What did you wish for?"
于是鲍比把一切都告诉了他们，每个人听了之后都一言不发。
鲍比说完后羞愧地低下了头。
“我一直在想，如果我是家里唯一的孩子会是什么样子。” 过了一会潘瑟说。“我告诉你，那一点儿意思都没有。” 鲍比深有感触地说。
So Bobby told them everything, and everyone listened and didn't say a word. After Bobby finished, he hung his head in shame.
"I've always wondered what it would be like to be an only child," Panther said after a while.
"It's no fun, I tell you!" Bobby said emphatically.
“我希望你现在珍惜像我这么好的哥哥。” 赛伊很夸张地张开了他的双臂。可是他不小心打翻了牛奶。牛奶全部洒在了简的身上，简大声尖叫起来。
"I hope you appreciate what a great brother I am now," said Cy, spreading his arms dramatically. As he did this, he accidentally knocked over the milk. It splashed all over Jane, and she screamed loudly.
“赛伊！” 潘瑟用妈妈般的口气说。
鲍比笑了，一切都恢复了平常的样子。
第二天一早，简非常激动。她催着每个人赶快吃完早饭。今天轮到她许愿了。
"Oh, Cy," Panther said in a motherly voice.
Bobby grinned. Everything was back to normal again.
The next morning, Jane was very excited. She hurried everyone through breakfast. It was her turn to make a wish.
“你仔细思考过你的愿望了吗？” 潘瑟担心地问。
“当然啦！” 简不耐烦地说。
“也许你应该先告诉我们你要许什么样的愿望。” 赛伊建议说。
"Have you thought carefully about your wish?" Panther asked in a worried voice.
"Of course," Jane said impatiently.
"Maybe you should tell us what your wish is first," suggested Cy.
“不！” 简说着摇摇头，“给你们一个惊喜。” 
其他的孩子看了看彼此。在经历了过去几天的事情之后，他们并没有像一开始那么积极去许愿了。
“来吧！” 简说着朝大门走去。
"No," Jane said, shaking her head. "It's a surprise."
The other children looked at each other. After their past experiences, they weren't as eager to make wishes as they had been before.
"Come on," Jane said and headed toward the door.
他们到井边的时候，鲍比屏住了呼吸。他记得昨天木桶动都不动。但是，赛伊很轻松地就用绳子顺利地把木桶拉了上来，休伊的壳就在桶里。
When they got to the well, Bobby held his breath. He remembered that the bucket would not move the day before. But Cy had no trouble as the rope smoothly pulled up the bucket, with Huey's shell inside.
孩子们呼唤着休伊。过了几分钟，休伊终于把他的脑袋探了出来。
“在你们开口之前，” 他开始说话了，“我要再一次提醒你们。” 他伸出了两根手指：“第一，你们许的任何愿望都会在日落时失效。第二，你们许完愿后就不可以再来打扰我了。” 
The children called for Huey for a few minutes before he finally stuck his head out. "Before you say anything," he started, "let me remind you once again." He lifted two fingers. "One, whatever you wish for ends at sunset. Two, after you make a wish you must not bother me."
他盯了鲍比好一会儿。鲍比低头看着自己的脚。“我不会让愿望失效，” 休伊继续说道，“所以你们在许愿之前必须考虑好。” 
除了简以外的每个人看上去都忧心忡忡的样子。
潘瑟和其他人希望简在许愿之前再想一想。
He stared at Bobby for a long time. Bobby looked down at his feet. "I cannot undo your wish," continued Huey, "so you have to think before you wish it."
Everyone except Jane looked worried. Panther and the others wanted Jane to think before making a wish.
“但这是我的愿望。” 简自己微笑着说。“我现在可以许愿了吗？休伊？” 她激动地小声说。
只有她按自己想的许愿才公平。
简双手合十，闭上了眼睛。她说：“我希望我们拥有翅膀。” 
"But it's my wish," Jane said, smiling to herself. "Can I make my wish now, Huey?" she whispered excitedly.
And it was only fair that she wish for whatever she wanted.
Jane clasped her hands together and, closing her eyes, she said, "I wish we had wings."
赛伊、潘瑟和鲍比盯着简。
“你刚刚是许愿要翅膀吗？” 赛伊不敢相信地问。
简的眼里涌出了泪花，她点点头。“我一直都很想飞。” 
突然间每个人都开始说话了。
Cy, Panther, and Bobby stared at Jane.
"Did you just wish for wings?" Cy asked in disbelief.
Jane's eyes welled up with tears as she nodded. "I've always wanted to fly."
Suddenly everyone started talking at once.
“嗯嗯。” 休伊清了清嗓子。
没人注意他。于是，休伊用力拍了拍手掌。
“不好意思，打扰一下。” 他说，“简的愿望是飞翔。现在取消她的愿望已经太迟了！” 休伊交叉双臂靠在了他的壳上。
"Ahem." Huey cleared his throat.
When nobody paid attention to him, Huey clapped his hands loudly.
"If I may interrupt," he said, "it is Jane's wish to fly. And it is too late to undo her wish!" Huey crossed his arms and leaned against his shell.
“噢，我的后背好痒。” 鲍比试图将手够到背后去挠痒痒。
“我也是。” 潘瑟说。
“怎么了？” 赛伊扭过头去看自己的背。
"Oh, my back is itchy." Bobby tried to reach behind him to scratch his back.
"Mine too," Panther said.
"What's going on?" Cy turned his head to look at his back.
两个白色的小包在他的背上鼓了起来。它们开始不断长大。很快孩子们看上去每个人的背上都背了一个巨大的白色的包。
“哦，我的天啊！” 潘瑟低声说，“我们有翅膀了。” 
她摆动着背上白色的翅膀。羽毛飞得到处都是。
Two white bumps appeared. Then they started to grow and grow and grow. Soon the children looked as if they had a huge white bag on each shoulder.
"Oh, my goodness," whispered Panther. "We have wings."
She wiggled the white humps on her back. Feathers flew everywhere.
“但我们要怎么才能飞？” 鲍比问。
休伊闭上了眼睛。“别看我。” 他说，“我的鳍又不是用来飞的。” 
休伊说着又消失在了他的壳里。
赛伊将那个壳送回了井底。与此同时，其他孩子正在努力试着使用他们的翅膀。
"But how do we fly?" asked Bobby.
Huey closed his eyes. "Don't look at me," he said. "My fins aren't for flying."
With that, Huey disappeared back into his shell.
Cy lowered the shell back into the well. Meanwhile, the others tried to use their wings.
鲍比闭着眼睛，握紧了拳头使劲，脸憋得通红。
“你看上去是需要去厕所。” 简咯咯笑道。
“好，那你来试试。” 鲍比涨红了脸说。
Bobby squeezed his eyes together. With his fists clenched, he made groaning noises.
"You look like you have to use the restroom," giggled Jane.
"Well, you try to do it then," Bobby said, his face turning red.
简闭上了双眼，静静地长长吐了一口气。然后，她背上的两个隆起物张开了，就像雨伞被打开那样。它们变成了美丽的天使翅膀。这些翅膀约有三米宽。
Jane closed her eyes and calmly let out a long breath. Then, like umbrellas unfolding, the two humps on her back opened up. They turned into beautiful angel wings. The wings were about three meters across.
“哇哦！” 其他的孩子们惊奇地大叫着。
简昂起头，弯曲了膝盖。然后，她用双脚轻轻蹬了一下地面。当简跳起来的时候，她的翅膀就开始拍打起来。
“我要飞了！我要飞了！” 简激动地叫着。
"Wow!" the others shouted in amazement.
Jane lifted her head and bent her knees. Then she pushed off with her feet. As she got higher, Jane's wings began to flap.
"I'm flying! I'm flying!" she screamed excitedly.
她的兄弟姐妹们也跟着她做。他们的翅膀一个接一个地打开了。不一会儿，他们就在天空中与简会合了。“简！” 潘瑟大叫，“这是我们到今天为止许过的最好的愿望！” 
简对着自己的姐姐微笑了一下，然后开始朝着太阳飞得更高了一些。
Her brothers and sister did exactly as Jane had done. One by one, their wings opened up and soon they joined their sister in the sky.
"Jane!" shouted Panther. "This is the best wish we've made so far."
Jane beamed at her sister as she flew up higher toward the sun.
“嘿，看！” 鲍比向下指着地面说。
在他们的下方是他们住的那个村子。孩子们可以看到人们在街道上走来走去。
“他们看上去和虫子一样小。” 赛伊笑了。
“那是玛莎阿姨的房子！” 潘瑟指着前方说。
"Hey, look!" Bobby pointed down at the ground.
Beneath them was their village. The children could see people walking up and down the streets.
"They look like bugs," laughed Cy.
"There's Aunt Martha's house!" Panther pointed ahead.
孩子们像一群鸟一样慢慢地朝着那个方向飞过去。
“玛莎阿姨在那儿呢。” 赛伊用一只手挡在眼睛上方向下看去。
“她在种花。” 潘瑟补充说。
“哦，那是我们的小宝宝，兰姆！” 简大叫。
Like a flock of birds, the children flew smoothly in that direction.
"There's Aunt Martha." Cy held one hand over his eyes as he peered down.
"She's planting some flowers," Panther added.
"Oh, and there's our baby Lamb!" shouted Jane.
他们清楚地看到兰姆坐在毯子上玩儿着他的玩具。
“玛莎阿姨！兰姆！” 鲍比突然开始挥舞着他的双臂大叫。
“鲍比！不要！” 赛伊抓住了鲍比的手臂。
Sure enough, Lamb was sitting on a blanket playing with his toys.
"Aunt Martha! Lamb!" Bobby suddenly began to shout and wave his arms.
"Bobby, stop it!" Cy grabbed Bobby's hand.
因为男孩子们在相互争论，他们忘记了继续拍打他们的翅膀。
“哇！” 赛伊和鲍比开始往下掉。
兰姆听见了熟悉的声音。“鲍比！赛伊！” 他说。
“你刚刚是叫了你的哥哥们的名字吗？” 玛莎阿姨自豪地问。
Because the boys were arguing, they forgot to keep flapping their wings.
"Whoa!" Cy and Bobby began falling.
Lamb heard familiar voices. "Bobba! Cy!" he said.
"Did you say your brothers' names?" Aunt Martha asked proudly.
“赛伊。鲍比。” 兰姆用他圆圆胖胖的小胳膊指着天空说。
“赛伊！鲍比！” 潘瑟和简大叫道。
“继续挥动你们的翅膀啊！” 
但男孩们却直直的往下落。
"Cy. Bobba." Lamb reached out his chubby arms to the sky.
"Cy! Bobby!" shouted Panther and Jane. "Keep moving your wings!"
But the boys kept falling and falling.
赛伊和鲍比向地面坠落的速度更快了。他们抓住了彼此。
“鲍比！” 赛伊大叫着，“拍打你的翅膀。一直拍打它们不要停！” 
这对兄弟很快重新掌握了飞翔的技巧。他们向上朝着姐妹们飞去。
“刚刚实在太惊险了。” 赛伊颤抖着声音说。
Cy and Bobby held on to each other as they fell faster to the ground.
"Bobby," cried Cy, "flap your wings. Keep them moving!"
Soon the brothers regained control. They flew over to their sisters.
"That was a close call," Cy said in a trembling voice.
潘瑟朝周围看了一圈儿。她发现了一根粗壮的树枝。“我们去那里休息一会吧。” 
孩子们朝着那棵树飞去。简突然问道：“我们要怎么降落？” 
“我们必须像收伞那样把翅膀收起来。” 过了一会儿潘瑟回答说，“我来试试看。” 
Panther looked around. She spotted a large tree branch. "Let's rest over there."
As the children flew toward the tree, Jane suddenly asked: "How do we land?"
"The wings have to fold back like an umbrella," Panther said after a while. "Let me try."
潘瑟朝着那棵树飞去。她的一只翅膀收了起来，但另一只却挂在了树叶上。
“啊！” 她大叫。
其他的孩子就跟在她后面。赛伊平稳地落在了树上。鲍比也没有什么问题。
Panther flew to the tree. One of her wings folded. The other, however, got caught in the leaves.
"Ouch!" she cried.
The other children followed after her. Cy landed gracefully, and so did Bobby.
“我们做到了！” 男孩子们喊道。他们咧开嘴笑了。
但简却并不想休息。
“我们没有很多时间了。” 她说，“我想尽可能地多飞一会儿。” 她昂起头，飞得更高了。
“我们最好待在一起。” 潘瑟说。
"We made it!" the brothers exclaimed, grinning.
Jane, however, did not want to rest.
"We don't have much time," she said. "I want to fly as much as I can." She lifted her head and flew higher.
"We'd better stay together," said Panther.
所以其他人也跟着简飞了起来。
孩子们飞过了湖泊和高山。孩子们飞过去的时候，鸟儿大声地发出了抗议。
过了好一会，鲍比说：“我饿了。” 
“但我们能去哪里弄点吃的呢？” 潘瑟大声地问。
So the others flew after Jane.
The children flew over lakes and mountains. Birds squawked loudly as the children flew by.
After a while, Bobby said, "I'm hungry."
"But where can we get food?" Panther wondered aloud.
“我们不能带着这些东西到处走啊。” 赛伊指着自己的翅膀说。
要把他们的翅膀藏起来是不可能做到的事情。
孩子们高高低低地飞着去寻找食物，但他们什么都没找到。
"We can't walk around with these things," said Cy. He pointed to his wings.
It would be impossible to hide their wings.
The children searched high and low for food. But they could not find anything.
“我知道我们去哪里可以找到吃的。” 赛伊说，“农夫詹金斯自己做果酱。” 农夫詹金斯是玛莎阿姨的邻居。
有时候他会给玛莎阿姨送自己家做的面包和果酱。
“你是说从他那里偷吃的？” 潘瑟大叫道。
"I know where we might find food," said Cy. "Farmer Jenkins makes his own jam." Farmer Jenkins was Aunt Martha's neighbor. Sometimes he gave Aunt Martha homemade bread and jam.
"You mean steal his food?" shouted Panther.
赛伊脸红了。“不是偷。我们只是借。” 
“这个主意太糟糕了。” 潘瑟指责说。
“你有更好的主意吗？” 赛伊反击说。
潘瑟当然什么主意都没想出来。
“好吧，如果你保证，我们会把拿的东西再还回去的话。” 她最终同意了。
Cy's face turned red. "Not steal it. Just borrow it."
"That's a terrible idea," scolded Panther.
"Do you have any better ones?" Cy shot back.
Of course Panther did not have any ideas at all.
"Well, if you promise to return what we take," she finally agreed.
“好，那就跟我来吧。” 赛伊转身飞走了。
他们飞到农场的时候，已经接近傍晚了。
“他可能将食物放在谷仓里了。” 赛伊说。
谷仓有两层。幸运的是，第二层有扇很大的窗户是开着的。孩子们从那扇窗飞了进去。
"Okay, just follow me." Cy turned around and flew off.
By the time they reached the farm, it was late afternoon.
"He probably keeps his food in the barn," said Cy.
The barn had two floors. Luckily, a large window on the second level was open. The children flew in through that window.
赛伊发现了几个木箱。它们装满了果酱。孩子们一句话都没说就开始吃果酱了。
“这真好吃。” 鲍比舔着嘴唇说。
“我们必须记清楚我们吃了多少。” 潘瑟边说边数空果酱罐。
Cy found several wooden boxes. They were filled with jam. Without a word, the children ate the jam.
"That was delicious," Bobby said, licking his lips.
"We must remember how much we ate," said Panther as she counted the empty jars.
“看！太阳开始落山了！” 简指着闪耀着红色光芒的天空说。
孩子们突然感觉自己变轻了，就好像他们的肩膀上少了些重量似的。
“翅膀不见了。” 简轻声说。她在自己平滑的后背上四处摸着。
"Look! The sun is setting!" Jane pointed to the glowing red sky.
Suddenly the children felt lighter, as if a weight had been lifted off their shoulders.
"They're gone," whispered Jane, reaching around to touch her flat back.
“好吧，我们现在必须从这里出去了。” 赛伊说。
但没有翅膀，孩子们就不能再飞出去了。大门在一楼。那里都是动物。
“我们可以从这个梯子上爬下去。” 赛伊说。
"Well, we have to get out of here now," Cy said.
Without wings, the children could not fly out. The main door was on the ground floor. The animals were kept down there.
"We can climb down this ladder," said Cy.
这一层的中间，有一个大大的正方形开口。开口里放着一架梯子。赛伊爬到了梯子上。
“来吧！” 赛伊边说边往下爬。
每个孩子都到了一楼后，动物们开始变得吵嚷起来。
In the middle of the floor was a large square opening. There was a ladder inside the opening. Cy got on the ladder.
"Come on," Cy said as he climbed down.
As each child climbed down, the animals grew noisier.
“嘘！” 潘瑟对着一头叫得特别欢的马小声说。
赛伊朝着谷仓大门走去。他拉了拉门。门一点儿都没开。他又试了一次。然后，赛伊睁大了双眼看着他的兄弟姐妹们。
“门锁上了。” 
"Hush," Panther whispered to a particularly loud horse.
Cy headed for the barn entrance. He pulled the door. It wouldn't move. He tried again. Cy looked at his siblings with wide eyes.
"It's locked."
谷仓的门被人从外面紧紧地锁住了。孩子们听到了农夫詹金斯的声音。
“谷仓里可能来了郊狼。” 他说。
“也许有人想偷咱们的马。” 詹金斯的儿子回答说。
The barn door was tightly locked from the outside. Then the children heard Farmer Jenkins' voice.
"It might be a coyote," he said.
"Or maybe someone's trying to steal our horses," the farmer's son replied.
“拿上枪，以防万一。” 农夫詹金斯叫道。
“枪？” 赛伊倒吸了一口冷气。
“我们得想想办法。” 潘瑟低声说。
孩子们四处看了看。没有地方可以让他们藏起来。就在这个时候，农夫詹金斯离谷仓越来越近了。
"Bring the guns, just in case," Farmer Jenkins shouted.
"Guns?" Cy gulped.
"We have to do something," Panther whispered.
The children looked around. There was no place to hide. Meanwhile Farmer Jenkins was getting closer.
“我很怕。” 简哭了起来。潘瑟朝着梯子跑过去。
“我有主意了。” 潘瑟说。“我们爬回到二楼去。” 
其他人在想她要干什么。
詹金斯和他的儿子已经站在了门外。“这是钥匙，爸。” 儿子说。他抖了抖钥匙。
"I'm scared," cried Jane just as Panther ran toward the ladder.
"I have an idea!" Panther said. "Climb back up to the second floor."
The others wondered what she was up to.
The farmer and his son were standing just outside the door. "Here's the key to the lock, Pa," said the son. He rattled a bunch of keys.
“我猜我们没有其他选择了。” 赛伊悄声说。
他跟在潘瑟后面爬回了二层。鲍比和简迅速地跟上了他们。
“帮我把这个拉上来。” 潘瑟命令说。
潘瑟和赛伊把梯子拉了上去。他们把梯子拖到了打开的窗户那里。
"I guess we have no choice," whispered Cy.
He climbed back up after Panther. Bobby and Jane quickly followed.
"Help me pull this up," Panther ordered.
Panther and Cy pulled the ladder up. They dragged it to the open window.
“我现在明白了。” 赛伊笑了。
年纪大一点儿的孩子们将梯子从窗户伸了出去。然后，他们将梯子靠在了谷仓的外墙上。
“快来，简！” 赛伊叫，“从梯子上爬下去。” 
“我要是掉下去怎么办？” 她用力吸了口气。
"I get it now," smiled Cy.
The older children lowered the ladder out from the window. Then they leaned it against the barn.
"Come on, Jane!" Cy shouted. "Climb down the ladder."
"What if I fall?" She sniffed.
“你不会掉下去的，我会拉着你。” 他向她保证说。
简爬了出去，顺着梯子下去了。然后，潘瑟跟着她下去了。
一楼，农夫詹金斯和他儿子正在开最后一把锁。
"You won't. I've got you," he assured her.
Jane climbed out and down the ladder. Then Panther followed her.
Downstairs, Farmer Jenkins and his son were just unlocking the last lock.
“不管谁在里面，我都听到你的声音了！” 农夫詹金斯大叫，“你逃不了了！” 
轮到鲍比了。他迅速跟上了她的姐妹们。突然，谷仓大门朝两侧飞快地打开了。大门发出了一声巨响。赛伊可以听到农夫詹金斯和他儿子在楼下的声音。
"Whoever is in there, I can hear you!" Farmer Jenkins shouted. "You can't escape!"
It was Bobby's turn. He quickly joined his sisters. Suddenly the barn door flew open. It made a thundering sound. Cy could hear Farmer Jenkins and his son down below.
“我知道你在这儿。” 农夫詹金斯用生气的声音说。
“赛伊！赛伊！” 他的兄弟姐妹们在谷仓外压低声音拼命地叫着。
赛伊做了一个深呼吸，然后爬了下去。孩子们头都没回地以最快速度跑走了。
"I know you're in here," Farmer Jenkins shouted in an angry voice.
"Cy! Cy!" his siblings whispered frantically from outside.
Cy took a deep breath and made his way down. Without looking back, the children ran as fast as they could.
回家后，玛莎阿姨生气了。“看看现在外面天黑成什么样了。” 她斥责道。
“我们很抱歉。” 潘瑟说。
“我太生气了。上楼回房间去！” 玛莎阿姨抱起了兰姆。
Back home, Aunt Martha was not happy with the children. "Look how dark it is outside," she scolded.
"We're sorry," apologized Panther.
"I'm very upset with you. Go upstairs to your rooms!" Aunt Martha picked up Lamb.
“鲍巴？赛伊？嘻嘻！嘻嘻！” 兰姆开心地大叫。他张开了双臂，仿佛那是他的翅膀，然后摆出了要飞的样子。
“也许兰姆真的看到我们了。” 鲍比低声说。
孩子们看看彼此笑了。
“最后我们还是没有挨饿啊。” 赛伊拍了拍自己的肚子。
"Bobba? Cy? Whee! Whee!" Lamb cried happily. He opened his arms as if they were wings and pretended to fly.
"Maybe Lamb did see us," Bobby whispered.
The children looked at each other and smiled.
"At least we're not hungry." Cy patted his stomach.
互相说了晚安后，孩子们回到了自己的房间。
第二天一早，玛莎阿姨给孩子们做了烤薄饼。“今天我要去见朋友。” 她说，“你们必须照顾小宝宝。” 她把兰姆抱给了潘瑟。
After saying good night, the children went to their rooms.
The next morning, Aunt Martha made pancakes for the children. "Today I'm meeting my friend," she said. "You'll need to look after the baby." She handed Lamb to Panther.
“我会在晚餐的时候回来。” 玛莎阿姨说。
所以，巴斯家的孩子们带着兰姆一起去见休伊。
“多可爱的小人儿啊！” 他充满热情地说。
兰姆指着休伊说：“猪。” 
"I'll be back at dinner time," Aunt Martha said.
So the Barth children took Lamb to see Huey.
"What an adorable little human!" he gushed.
Lamb pointed and said: "Pig."
“他说什么，猪？” 休伊的笑容消失了。
“不是，他，嗯，他说的是大。” 赛伊紧张地笑着，“哦， 轮到潘瑟许愿了。” 
但他的妹妹摇摇头说：“我不想许愿。” 
“为什么不呢？” 其他孩子问。
"Did he just call me a pig?" Huey's smile disappeared.
"No, he, uh, he said big." Cy laughed nervously. "Oh, it's Panther's turn to make a wish."
But his sister shook her head. "I don't want to."
"Why not?" asked the others.
“每次许愿之后总是会发生一些糟糕的事情。” 她说，“我觉得我们不应该再来见休伊了。” 
“好吧，我有个愿望。” 赛伊说。
“那你说吧。” 休伊说。
“我希望我们可以见到恐龙。” 
"Something terrible always happens after every wish," she said. "I think we should stop coming to see Huey."
"Well, I have a wish to make," said Cy.
"Go on then," said Huey.
"I wish we could see dinosaurs."
大家都惊呆了，就连兰姆都变得安静了。
“你刚才说什么，恐龙吗？” 鲍比重复道。
“我一直都想见到真正的恐龙。” 赛伊解释说。
休伊清了清嗓子，指向那口井。
Everyone except Cy was stunned. Even Lamb grew quiet.
"Did you say dinosaurs?" repeated Bobby.
"I've always wanted to see a real one," Cy explained.
Huey cleared his throat and pointed to the well.
“但我的愿望还没实现呢。” 赛伊争论道。
休伊交叉了双臂问：“到现在为止还有什么愿望没实现吗？” 
孩子们承认说实际上每个愿望都实现了。
“那这个愿望也会的。” 休伊消失在了他的壳里。
"But my wish hasn't come true yet," argued Cy.
Crossing his arms, Huey asked, "Has any wish not come true so far?"
The children admitted that every wish had indeed come true.
"This one will too." Huey disappeared into his shell.
赛伊把他送回了井底，喃喃自语道：“我一只恐龙都没看到。” 
“耐心一点！” 休伊在他的壳里喊着。
“我们回家吧。” 赛伊用失望的声音说。
孩子们开始往家走，但情况有点儿不一样了。
“我以前从没有看到过这棵树。” 简指着一棵高高的长满了叶子的树。
Cy lowered him down, mumbling, "I don't see any dinosaurs."
"Patience!" Huey yelled from his shell.
"Let's go home," Cy said in a disappointed voice.
The children started to walk back home. But something was different.
"I've never seen this tree before." Jane pointed to a tall leafy tree.
“这条路看上去很陌生。” 潘瑟大声说着搂紧了兰姆。
“那棵树为什么在动？” 鲍比跟在他哥哥身后走了几步。
一棵树上的叶子摆动个不停。然后，孩子们感觉到地面在颤动。
"This road looks unfamiliar," Panther remarked, holding Lamb close to her.
"Why is that tree moving?" Bobby took steps behind his brother.
The leaves on one tree swayed side to side. Then the children felt the ground tremble.
“有东西朝着这边过来了！” 赛伊激动地压低了声音说。
“那是什么？” 简问。她都快哭出来了。
突然，一只长着很多角的巨大恐龙出现在了树林里。
“那是犀牛。” 鲍比害怕地说。
"Something's coming this way," whispered Cy excitedly.
"What is it?" Jane asked, close to tears.
Suddenly a huge dinosaur with many horns appeared among the trees.
"It's a rhino," Bobby said in awe.
那只巨大的恐龙跟犀牛很像，但它比犀牛大的多，也可怕的多。
“不，那不是犀牛。” 赛伊纠正说。
“那是尖角龙。” 
那只尖角龙并不知道孩子们在看着它。它继续吃着树上的叶子。
The huge dinosaur did resemble a rhinoceros in many ways. But it was much bigger and scarier.
"No, that's not a rhino," Cy corrected. "That's a Centrosaurus."
The Centrosaurus did not know the children were watching him. He continued to eat the leaves off the trees and plants.
“它危险吗？” 简小声说。
赛伊点点头。“但如果我们不打扰它，我们就是安全的。” 
“所以休伊实现了你的愿望。” 潘瑟说。
就在那个时候，尖角龙抬起了头，在空气中嗅了嗅。它响亮地哼了一声，在地面上抓了一下。
"Is he dangerous?" Jane said in a tiny voice.
Cy nodded. "But if we don't bother him, we will be safe."
"So Huey gave you your wish," said Panther.
At that moment, the Centrosaurus lifted his head and sniffed the air. He grunted loudly and pawed the ground.
“哦！哦！” 赛伊说，“我觉得他闻到我们了。” 
“我们要怎么做？” 鲍比问。
“每个人都慢慢往后退。” 赛伊平静地说。
孩子们向后退到了森林深处。
"Uh-oh," said Cy, "I think he can smell us."
"What do we do?" asked Bobby.
"Everybody move back slowly. Slowly now," Cy said in a calm voice.
The children stepped back deeper into the woods.
安全地远离了恐龙后，赛伊激动地跳了起来。
“那一定是白垩纪。” 他说，“这些恐龙是活在 1.3 亿年前的。” 
孩子们饶有兴趣地睁大了眼睛，兰姆除外。他的眼皮快要合上了。
Safely away from the dinosaur, Cy jumped up in excitement.
"This must be the Cretaceous period," he said. "These dinosaurs lived over 130 million years ago."
The children's eyes grew wide with interest─except for Lamb's. His eyes were getting heavy.
“到他午睡时间了。” 
潘瑟低声说。兰姆很快就在怀里睡着了。赛伊在一棵树下发现了一个安全干燥的地方。潘瑟轻轻地把兰姆放在了那里。
“我们去看更多的恐龙吧。” 赛伊说。
“我们不能离开他。” 潘瑟态度坚决地说。
"It's his nap time," Panther whispered. Lamb was soon fast asleep in her arms. Cy found a safe, dry spot under a tree. Panther gently placed Lamb there.
"Let's go see more dinosaurs," Cy said.
"We can't leave him," argued Panther.
“我们不会走远的。” 赛伊说，“就在那棵树周围。” 
潘瑟承认看见恐龙这样巨大的生物是非常惊奇的。她看着兰姆，但却跟在了赛伊后面。
“那是禽龙。” 赛伊指着一只有着长长的僵硬的尾巴的动物说。
"We won't go far," said Cy. "Just around that tree."
Panther admitted that seeing the giant creatures was very fascinating. She kept a close eye on Lamb but followed Cy.
"That one is an Iguanodon," Cy said, pointing to a creature with a long, stiff tail.
“那个长了鸭嘴。” 简咯咯笑道。她指着一只手臂很短的高大恐龙。
“那是冠龙。” 赛伊说。
“他看上去像是戴了顶头盔。” 鲍比大笑着。他指着那只恐龙头上的冠。
"That one has a duck's bill," giggled Jane. She stared at a tall dinosaur with short arms.
"That's a Corythosaurus," Cy said.
"It looks like he's wearing a helmet." Bobby laughed aloud. He was referring to the crest on its head.
孩子们本来还可以再多看一会恐龙的，但可怕的事情发生了。
“赛伊，兰姆在哪里？” 潘瑟大叫。
他们回头去看兰姆睡觉的地方，但他不见了。
The children would have watched the dinosaurs longer, but something terrible had happened.
"Cy, where's Lamb?" Panther cried.
They looked back where Lamb had been sleeping. But he was gone.
潘瑟不停地哭着说：“都是我的错。” 
“我们会找到他的。” 赛伊安慰她说。
孩子们在找兰姆的时候，鲍比发现了一件有趣的事情。“脚印！” 他叫道。
Panther would not stop crying. "It's all my fault," she kept repeating.
"We're going to find him," comforted Cy.
As the children searched, Bobby discovered something interesting. "Footprints!" he shouted.
赛伊仔细地研究了那些脚印。然后他说：“这个生物可以用两只脚或者四只脚行走。” “是恐龙带走了兰姆？它如果把兰姆吃了怎么办？” 简担心地问。
Cy studied the prints carefully. Then he said, "This creature can walk on two or four legs."
"A dinosaur took Lamb? What if he eats him?" asked Jane, worried.
“我们不能再浪费时间了。” 赛伊跟着那些脚印走。这脚印把他们带到了丛林深处。
孩子们把每个地方都找遍了，但依然没有找到兰姆。他们精疲力尽地靠在一棵树上。
"We can't waste anymore time." Cy followed the footprints. They led them deeper into the forest.
They looked everywhere but still found no sign of Lamb. Exhausted, the children leaned against a tree.
“我们现在要怎么办？” 潘瑟抽泣着说。
突然，地面开始震动。孩子们看了看彼此。他们有点儿害怕。地面再次抖了抖。
“有个大家伙过来了。” 鲍比说。
"What are we going to do now?" sniffed Panther.
Suddenly the ground began to shake. The children glanced at each other. They were afraid. The ground trembled again.
"Something really big is coming," said Bobby.
然后，他们听到了全世界最恐怖的吼声。他们觉得毛骨悚然。

“她以为我当了妈妈了！” 简依然很生气。
“但是简，” 潘瑟温柔地说，“你现在已经是个成年人了。” 
还没等简回答，他们就听到了一阵熟悉的声音。
“那是冰淇淋车！” 鲍比跳个不停。
"What did that woman say to you?" asked Cy.
"She thought I was a mother!" Jane was still upset.
"But Jane," Panther said gently. "You are grown-up now."
Before Jane could answer, they heard familiar sounds.
"It's the ice cream truck!" Bobby jumped up and down.
“因为咖啡实在太难喝了。” 赛伊咧着嘴笑，“我们应该来点甜的东西。” 
他们跑着穿过了公园。因为他们的体格更大，速度更快，他们超过了所有的小孩子，第一个到达了冰淇淋车那里。
“双份焦糖巧克力软糖，谢谢。” 鲍比搓着手说。
"Since coffee was so yucky," grinned Cy, "we deserve something sweet!"
The grown-up Barths raced across the park. Being bigger and faster, they passed all the little children. They got to the ice cream truck first.
"Double caramel chocolate fudge, please," said Bobby, rubbing his hands.
“对不起！” 司机冷冷地说，“应该让孩子们先买。” 
他指着他们身后那些排队的孩子们说。
“我先来的。” 鲍比固执地回答。有些父母开始非常不客气地批评他。“那个大男人真不怕羞。” 
"Excuse me," the driver said coldly. "Maybe the children should go first."
He pointed to the line of children behind the Barths.
"I got here first," Bobby replied stubbornly.
Some parents made rude remarks.
"That grown man should be ashamed."
“他怎么跟孩子似的！” 
司机无奈地耸了耸肩，给了鲍比他的冰淇淋。
“我要一个草莓混合口味的。” 潘瑟说。
“你们是一起工作的吗？” 司机问她。
"He's acting like a child!"
The driver shrugged and gave Bobby his ice cream.
"I'll have a strawberry twist," Panther said.
"Do you all work together?" the driver asked her.
“不，我们是一家人。” 潘瑟一边从司机手上拿过冰淇淋一边回答。
“今天你们休息？” 他问。
“你是什么意思？” 潘瑟反问道。
“你们今天不工作？” 那个男人用愤怒的语气问。
“工作？我没有工作啊。” 她说，“我们都不工作。” 
"No, we're family," she replied as she took the ice cream from him.
"Is today your day off?" he asked.
"What do you mean?" Panther asked in return.
"You're not working today?" the man said in an irritated voice.
"Work? I don't have a job," she said. "None of us do."
那个男人带着厌恶表情看着她。
“你应该为自己感到羞愧！” 他说，“所有成年人都在努力工作去挣钱，但你们......” 他指着巴斯家的每个孩子：“你们就只想要冰淇淋。” 
The man looked at her with a nasty expression.
"You should be ashamed of yourself!" he said. "All grown-ups work hard to make money. But you─" He pointed to each of the Barths. "─you only want ice cream."
突然间冰淇淋就不再那么吸引人了。巴斯家的孩子们看看周围，只看到了一张张愤怒的脸。
“我们可能该走了。” 赛伊说。
他们低着头离开了。
他们找到一张没人的长椅后，简问：“人们为什么对我们这么刻薄？” 
Suddenly the ice cream no longer seemed inviting. The Barths looked around and saw angry faces.
"Maybe we should go," said Cy.
They hung their heads and turned away.
"Why are people so mean?" Jane asked after they found a quiet bench.
“因为我们的举止像孩子一样。” 潘瑟回答。
“但我们就是孩子啊。” 简争辩说。
“今天不是的，简！我们今天是成年人，记得吗？” 赛伊说，“在每个人的眼里，我们都是行为像孩子一样的成年人。” 
"Because we acted like children," replied Panther.
"But we are children," argued Jane.
"Not today, Jane! We're grown-ups today, remember?" said Cy. "Everyone sees us as grown-ups who act like children."
巴斯家的孩子们沉默了。他们看着其他的孩子们玩耍。
“他们看上去真开心。” 鲍比说，“我也想骑自行车。” 
“我以前觉得当大人会很有意思。” 简用力吸了口气，“我不知道当大人会这么难。” 
The Barth kids were silent. They watched other children playing.
"They look so happy," said Bobby. "I wish I could ride a bike, too."
"I thought being a grown-up would be fun," Jane sniffed. "I didn't know it would be so hard."
他们坐在长椅上，无事可做。赛伊看了看手表，说：“我们回玛莎阿姨家吧。” 他说：“我们可以藏起来等日落。” 
The Barths sat on the bench for a while. They didn't know what else to do. Then Cy looked at his watch. "Let's go back to Aunt Martha's," he said. "We can hide until the sun sets."
“我穿着这双鞋没法再走路了。” 简嚷嚷着。她把高跟鞋扔到了地上。
“好吧，简。” 赛伊说，“我来背你。” 
简爬到了赛伊的背上。
"I can't walk in these shoes anymore," cried Jane. She threw her heels on the ground.
"Okay, Jane," Cy said, "I'll carry you."
Jane climbed onto Cy's back.
“哇！你好重！” 他喘着气说。
但赛伊还是像个好哥哥那样背着他的妹妹穿过了公园。
路过的人们都停下来盯着他们。“哦，我的天哪！” 
他们小声说：“他们在干吗？” 
“他们不觉得丢人吗？” 
"Whoa! You're heavy!" he gasped.
But like a good brother, he carried his sister through the park.
People passing by stopped and stared. "Oh, my!" they muttered. "What are they doing?"
"They should be ashamed of themselves."
巴斯家的孩子们到家时，天已经开始黑了。
“我不想长大了。” 简说。她的嘴唇颤抖着。
“长大不是问题。” 潘瑟温柔地说，“问题是我们长得太快了。我们还没准备好。” 
When the Barths got home, it was getting dark.
"I don't ever want to grow up," Jane said, her lips trembling.
"Growing up is not the problem," Panther said kindly. "It's growing up too fast. We weren't ready."
“好吧。我觉得这里的人就是很刻薄。” 鲍比说，“在芝加哥，没人会说那么让人讨厌的话。” 
“是你不知道而已，鲍比。” 赛伊说。
“不，我知道。这个镇上的人都很刻薄，而且这里太无聊了！” 鲍比继续说，“如果没有休伊的话，我们在这里根本没事情可做。” 
"Well, I think people here are just mean," said Bobby. "In Chicago, nobody would have said nasty things."
"You don't know that, Bobby," said Cy.
"Yes, I do. This town is full of mean people. And it's so boring!" Bobby continued. "If it weren't for Huey, we would have nothing to do."
孩子们同意了鲍比的说法。
“我觉得今天的愿望对我们来说是个很好的教训。” 潘瑟说。
“至少我们知道了我们长大以后会是什么样子。” 赛伊大笑着拍拍自己的肚子。
The children agreed with him on that.
"I think today's wish was a good lesson for us," said Panther.
"At least we know what we're going to look like," laughed Cy as he patted his stomach.
突然，孩子们出现了和早上一样四肢发痒的感觉。他们的胳膊和腿变短了。赛伊的肚子消失了。鲍比的小胖肚子却又长出来了。几分钟后，他们又恢复了孩子的样子。
“重新回到孩子的样子真好。” 简说。
Suddenly the Barths felt the same tickling feeling they had felt that morning. Their arms and legs got shorter. Cy's stomach disappeared while Bobby's reappeared. Within a few minutes, the Barths were once again children.
"It's good to be me again," said Jane.
他们互相拥抱。
“我们进去吃点东西吧。” 潘瑟说。
吃晚饭的时候，玛莎阿姨告诉了孩子们她今天做的事情。
“我带着兰姆去了公园。” 她说，“我们看到了几个大人在公园里到处跑。他们的行为就像孩子一样。” 
They gave each other hugs.
"Let's go inside and have something to eat," said Panther.
During dinner, Aunt Martha told them about her day.
"I took Lamb to the park," she said. "We saw these adults running around behaving like children."
“也许他们只是想玩儿玩儿。” 赛伊轻声说。
“玩儿玩儿没关系，但人们的举止应该和他们的年纪相符！” 玛莎阿姨说。
孩子们看了看彼此。
“我同意玛莎阿姨的说法。” 简说，“我们的举止应该与我们的年龄相符。” 
"Maybe they just wanted to have fun," Cy said in a quiet voice.
"Having fun is no problem. But people should act their age!" Aunt Martha said.
The children looked at each other.
"I agree with Aunt Martha," said Jane. "We should act our age."
第二天一早，孩子们做好了外出的准备。玛莎阿姨带着兰姆去了花园。
“今天轮到你了，鲍比。” 赛伊耳语道。
“我没有愿望啊。” 他说，“我能明天再许愿吗？” 
其他孩子想了想。然后，潘瑟说：“我们每人都可以跳过一次。” 
The next morning, the children got ready to go out. Aunt Martha took Lamb outside to the garden.
"It's your turn, Bobby," whispered Cy.
"I don't have a wish," he said. "Can I do it tomorrow?"
The others thought about it. Then Panther said, "We can all use a break."
“那我们今天不去见休伊了。” 赛伊说。
潘瑟读了一本书。简玩儿玩具。赛伊去骑车了。鲍比什么都没干。他不想出去，也不喜欢读书，更不想玩玩具。
“这里太无聊了。” 他一直嘟囔着。
"So today we won't go see Huey," said Cy.
Panther read a book. Jane played with her dolls. Cy went for a bike ride. And Bobby did nothing. He didn't want to go outside. He hated reading. He didn't want to play with his toys.
"It's so boring here," he kept saying.
“那你为什么不找点事情做呢？” 潘瑟说。
“这里没事情可做啊。这个镇子太无聊了！” 他抱怨说，“我想念芝加哥。那里总是有很多好玩儿的事情。” 
“鲍比，我们最近有过很多次探险了。” 潘瑟说，“你还觉得无聊吗？” 
"Why don't you do something then?" said Panther.
"There's nothing to do. This town is so boring!" he complained. "I miss Chicago. Something exciting always happens there."
"Bobby, we've had so many adventures lately," said Panther. "And you're still bored?"
“我希望这里发生点有意思的事。在这儿，在镇子上。” 鲍比说。
潘瑟转了转眼珠，然后又埋头去读书了。
突然，鲍比坐了起来打了个响指。“对了！” 他叫道，“我知道我要许什么愿了。” 
"I want something exciting to happen here! Here in this town," said Bobby emotionally.
Panther rolled her eyes and returned to her book.
Suddenly Bobby sat up and snapped his fingers. "That's it!" he shouted. "I know what I'm going to wish for."
第二天，鲍比的心情好多了。
“昨天没许愿挺好的。” 赛伊说。
“对，我终于读完我的书了。” 潘瑟说。
“我不用去担心恐龙的事情。也不用管那些刻薄的人。” 简咯咯笑道。
The next day, Bobby was in a better mood.
"It was nice not having a wish yesterday," said Cy.
"Yeah, I finally finished my book," said Panther.
"I didn't have to worry about dinosaurs or mean people," giggled Jane.
“好，我今天准备好许愿了。” 鲍比激动地说。
因为某些原因，其他人并没有那么积极。但他们还是去了休伊的那口井。赛伊把他拉上来的时候，休伊并没有马上从壳里出来。孩子们不得不轻轻敲了敲他的壳。
"Well, I'm ready to make my wish today," Bobby said excitedly.
For some reason, the others did not feel as eager. But they went to Huey's well. When Cy pulled him up, Huey did not come out right away. They had to tap lightly on his shell.
“哦！是你们啊！我以为你们不会再来了呢。” 休伊说。
赛伊解释说他们前一天没有想出什么愿望。
“但今天我有愿望了。” 鲍比满脸笑容地说。“我希望，” 他用戏剧性的语调说，“我们的镇上发生一些有趣的事情。” 
"Oh, it's you! I thought you weren't coming back," Huey said when he appeared.
Cy explained that they hadn't come up with a wish the previous day.
"But today I have," beamed Bobby. "I wish," he said dramatically, "that something exciting would happen in our town."
鲍比和他的兄弟姐妹们走回了家。到目前为止一切如常。
“你希望发生什么有趣的事情？” 潘瑟问。
“我不在乎是什么事情。” 鲍比耸耸肩。
Bobby and his siblings walked back home. So far everything seemed the same.
"What exciting thing were you thinking of?" asked Panther.
"Anything! I don't care." Bobby shrugged.
他们到家的时候，玛莎阿姨正要出门。“我需要去趟银行。” 她说。
“我们能和你一起去吗？” 简问。
“简！” 鲍比嘘了一声。
他想等着自己的愿望成真。
When they got home, Aunt Martha was on her way out. "I need to run to the bank," she said.
"Can we come with you?" asked Jane.
"Jane!" hissed Bobby.
He wanted to wait for his wish to come true.
“当然。” 玛莎阿姨说，“然后，我们去给兰姆买新鞋子。” 
“鞋子？” 鲍比几乎叫出来。这一天听上去确实没什么好玩儿的。
孩子们和玛莎阿姨一起去了银行。鲍比没精打采地拖着脚走在最后。
"Of course," Aunt Martha said. "Then we can get new shoes for Lamb."
"Shoes?" Bobby almost shouted. This day did not sound exciting at all.
The children walked with Aunt Martha to the bank. Bobby walked behind the others and dragged his feet.
银行的警卫迎接了他们。“早上好，各位。” 
“早上好。” 他们回应说。
那天早上银行里相当忙碌。玛莎阿姨抱着兰姆去排队了。
The guard at the bank greeted them. "Good morning, everyone."
"Good morning," they said in return.
The bank was quite busy that morning. Aunt Martha, holding Lamb, went to stand in line.
“你们可以在长椅这等我。” 她告诉孩子们。
孩子们在木头椅子上坐下了。他们看看四周。鲍比看到了那个店主，基弗先生。他还看到了贝克尔先生。鲍比又朝银行外面看去。
"You can wait for me on those benches," she told the children.
The children sat on hard wooden benches. They looked around. Bobby noticed Mr. Keefer, the shop owner. He also saw Mr. Becker. Bobby glanced outside.
贝尔克先生的狗西尔弗被拴在了一个消防栓上。就在这时，四个男人在银行门前停下了。他们看上去很紧张。一个男人的脸上有道伤疤。另一个则扎着马尾。
“我在想他们是不是丢东西了。” 鲍比大声说。
Mr. Becker's dog, Silver, was tied to a fire hydrant. At that moment, four men stopped in front of the bank. They looked nervous. One man had a scar on his face. Another man had a ponytail.
"I wonder if they're lost," Bobby said aloud.
然后，他们就快的像闪电一般拉下了戴在头上的面具。鲍比张大了嘴，但却什么也说不出来。那几个男人拿着枪走进了银行。
“女士们，先生们！” 一个男人平静地说。
Then quick as lightning, they pulled masks over their heads. Bobby opened his mouth but no sounds came out. The men entered the bank holding guns.
"Ladies and gentlemen," one man said in a calm voice.
银行里的顾客都转过身来。当他们看到那些带着面具的男人时，有人开始尖叫。
“各位，请保持冷静。不要尖叫！” 那个男人举起了枪说。
The bank customers turned around. When they saw the masked men, some began screaming.
"Everybody, stay calm. Do not scream!" the man said, holding his gun up.
这时，另一个男人把警卫的双手绑在了背后，嘴上也贴上了胶带。第三个的男人锁上了银行的大门。他快速拉下了每扇窗子的百叶帘。第四个男人则跳到了出纳员的身后。
Meanwhile, another man tied the guard's hands behind his back. He put tape over his mouth too. A third man locked the bank doors. He rushed around pulling down the blinds. The fourth man jumped behind the tellers.
“别按警铃！” 他警告他们说。“我希望每个人都到这里来。” 第一个男人说。他指着孩子们坐的地方。“然后，趴到地上。” 
"Do not push the alarm!" he warned them.
"I want everyone to move here," the first man said. He pointed to where the children sat. "And lie down on the floor."
“拜托，这里有孩子。” 一个女人叫道。那是玛莎阿姨。
“安静！” 第一个男人大叫。他可能是老大。“如果他们听话，” 他转过去看着巴斯家的孩子们说，“就不会有人受伤的。” 
"Please, there are children here," a woman cried. It was Aunt Martha.
"Quiet!" the first man shouted. He was probably the leader. "If they behave," he said, turning to look at the Barth children, "nobody will get hurt."
每个人都听从了这些强盗的指令。他们走到孩子们坐着的地方，然后趴在了地上。玛莎阿姨打手势让孩子们也趴在地上。
Everybody obeyed the robber's instructions. They walked to where the children sat. Then they dropped to the floor. Aunt Martha motioned for the children to lie on the ground as well.
鲍比在地板上只能看到强盗们的脚。有两个男人穿着运动鞋。其中一只运动鞋上有个大洞。鲍比可以看到那个男人穿的袜子。
那是红蓝杠的。
From the floor, Bobby could see only the robbers' feet. Two of the men wore sneakers. One of the sneakers had a large hole in it. Bobby could see the socks of the man wearing it. They were blue with red stripes.
“把你们的钱和首饰都放到这个袋子里。” 穿着红蓝杠袜子的强盗在人们面前扔下了一个袋子。人们掏出了自己的钱包和皮夹。有些取下了自己的手表。强盗们还拿走了戒指、项链和耳环。
"Put all your money and jewelry in this bag." The robber with blue socks dropped a bag in front of the people. They removed their purses and wallets. Some took off their watches. The robbers also took rings, necklaces, and earrings.
鲍比微微抬起了头。
在柜台后面，出纳员打开了他们的存钱柜。“快点。” 第四个男人说着拿枪靠近了他们。
“怎么样，鲍比？” 赛伊对着他低声说，“你的愿望成真了。” 
Bobby lifted his head slightly.
Behind the counter, the tellers opened their registers. "Hurry up," the fourth man said, holding the gun close to them.
"Well, Bobby," Cy whispered to him, "your wish came true."
趴在地板上非常不舒服。
“你还好吗？” 一个男人低声对鲍比说。
那是农夫詹金斯。“会没事的。” 他安慰着鲍比。
Lying on the floor was very uncomfortable.
"Are you okay?" a man whispered to Bobby.
It was Farmer Jenkins. "Everything's going to be fine," he assured Bobby.
“所有人都闭上眼睛。” 领头的强盗大叫道，“然后从一数到一百。” 
每个人都照做了。鲍比听见了重重的脚步声。然后，他听到某个地方的门打开了。
“他们走了！” 一个女人大声说。
"I want everyone to close their eyes," the leader shouted. "Then start counting to one hundred."
Everyone did exactly as he said. Bobby heard heavy footsteps. Then he heard a door opening somewhere.
"They're gone!" a woman said loudly.
“……五十七，五十八……” 简还在数。她依旧紧紧闭着眼睛。
潘瑟轻轻拍了拍她。“没事了，简。” 她说。
人们慢慢小心地从地上爬起来。外面响起了警笛声。很快，警察和一队救护人员就聚在了银行里。
“有没有谁看见了什么？” 有位警官大声叫道。
" . . . fifty-seven, fifty-eight . . . " Jane kept counting, her eyes still closed.
Panther gently tapped her. "It's over, Jane," she said.
People started to get up slowly and carefully. Police sirens rang loudly outside. Soon police officers and an ambulance crew crowded the bank.
"Did anybody see anything?" an officer called out.
大部分都摇了摇头。
“他们戴了面具。” 一个男人说。
鲍比举起了手。
“你是不是看到了什么，男孩儿？” 警官问鲍比说。
“是的，先生。” 鲍比胆怯地说。
Most of the people shook their heads.
"They wore masks," one man said.
Bobby raised his hand.
"Did you see something, son?" the officer asked Bobby.
"Yes, sir," Bobby answered timidly.
警官将他的手放在了鲍比的肩膀上，说：“告诉我你看到了什么。” 
鲍比描述了那些男人们进入银行之前的样子。他还告诉警官他注意到的其中一个男人穿的袜子。“但我没看到他们的脸。” 他说。
The officer put his hand on Bobby's shoulder and said, "Tell me what you saw."
Bobby described the men before they entered the bank. He also told the officer about the socks he'd noticed on one of the men. "I didn't see their faces though," he said.
警官给了鲍比一个大大的微笑。“这会帮助我们找到他们的。” 
鲍比骄傲地挺起了胸膛。
“你们是不是玛莎的侄子侄女？” 一个穿着黄色毛衣的女人走近了他们。孩子们点点头。
The officer gave Bobby a big smile. "This is going to help us find them."
Bobby's chest swelled with pride.
"Aren't you Martha's nieces and nephews?" A woman wearing a yellow sweater approached them. The children nodded.
“你们表现得非常勇敢。” 她称赞道。
孩子们害羞地笑了。
“我同意。” 贝尔克先生也走到了他们这边。
几个小时后，警方允许所有人回家了，但很多人都不想回家。
"You were very brave throughout this incident," she commended.
The children smiled shyly.
"I agree." Mr. Becker came over to them as well.
A few hours later, the police allowed everyone to return home. Many people, however, did not want to go home.
“以前从没发生过这样的事情。” 基弗先生生气地说。
“我们必须抓住这些强盗。” 贝尔克先生激动地说。
“但我们能做什么呢？” 玛莎阿姨问。
"Nothing like this has happened before," Mr. Keefer said angrily.
"We must catch those robbers!" Mr. Becker said emphatically.
"But what can we do?" asked Aunt Martha.
“我们应该一起合作保护我们的镇子。” 基弗先生说。
人们同意地点点头。玛莎阿姨邀请大家去她家里喝茶。
"We should all work together to protect our town," said Mr. Keefer.
People nodded in agreement. Aunt Martha invited everyone to her house for tea.
“我们喝点茶之后可以更好地思考。” 她说。孩子们帮助玛莎阿姨准备了茶水。他们还拿出了一些饼干。
"We can think better after a drink," she said.
The children helped Aunt Martha prepare the tea. They also brought out some cookies.
“谢谢你们。” 
“你们实在太乖了。” 
“你们真有礼貌！” 
人们不停地称赞孩子们。
“这里的每个人都很好。” 当孩子们待在厨房里时，潘瑟对大家说。
"Thank you."
"You're so sweet."
"What good manners you have!"
People kept complimenting the children.
"Everyone is so nice," Panther said when they were alone in the kitchen.
“有个女人说我看上去像她孙女。” 简咧开了嘴笑着说。
“贝克尔先生邀请我们去他家吃晚饭。” 赛伊说。
鲍比也开始喜欢这些邻居了。
“孩子们！” 玛莎阿姨走进了厨房。“你们能帮我再去买些面包吗？” 她问。
"One woman said I look like her granddaughter," beamed Jane.
"Mr. Becker wants us to come over for dinner," said Cy.
Bobby was also beginning to like his neighbors.
"Children," Aunt Martha came into the kitchen. "Can you get more bread for me?" she asked.
赛伊和鲍比自愿去跑腿。玛莎阿姨把钱给了他们之后说：“快点回来，路上小心！” 
男孩子们向面包店跑去。
“我听说那会儿你们在银行里。” 面包师对他们说。
面包师问了很多问题。一些顾客也问了些问题。
Cy and Bobby volunteered to go. Handing them some money, Aunt Martha said, "Hurry back and be careful!"
The boys left the house and ran over to the bakery.
"I heard you were at the bank," the baker said to them.
The baker asked many questions. Some of the customers did too.
“嗯。” 最后面包师说，“你们非常勇敢。我希望他们可以抓住这些强盗。” 
赛伊要付面包钱。
“这些多少钱？” 
面包师摇摇头说：“不用了，这些我请客。是送给你们这些勇敢的孩子的。” 
"Well," the baker said finally, "you were very brave. I hope they catch those robbers."
Cy took out the money to pay for the bread. "How much is this?"
The baker shook his head. "No, this is on me. For you brave
children."
男孩们向面包师道谢后就跑出去了。
“哦！” 鲍比直接撞到了一个路过的男人。鲍比跌在了地上。
“看着点儿路，小孩儿！” 那个男人对着鲍比大叫。
“你还好吗？” 赛伊把鲍比拉了起来。
Thanking him, the boys ran out.
"Oomph!" Bobby ran right into a man walking by. Bobby fell hard on the ground.
"Watch where you're going, kid!" he yelled at Bobby.
"Are you okay?" Cy asked, pulling Bobby up.
“赛伊！” 鲍比紧紧抓住了他哥哥的手臂，“看那个男人的鞋子！” 
那个男人就要过马路了。他的一只鞋子上有个洞。他穿着一双红蓝杠的袜子。
"Cy!" Bobby grabbed his brother's arm. "Look at that man's shoes!"
The man was about to cross the street. One of his shoes had a hole in it. And he wore blue socks with red stripes.
男孩儿们看着那个男人穿过了马路。
“我们必须跟着他。” 鲍比几乎叫了出来。
“我们该报警吧？” 赛伊不确定地说。
“没时间了。快来！” 看到左右都没车，鲍比跑过了马路。哥哥也跟了过去。
The boys watched as the man crossed the street.
"We have to follow him," Bobby almost shouted.
"Maybe we should find the police," Cy said uncertainly.
"We don't have time. Come on!" Bobby made sure the street was clear. Then he ran over to the other side. His brother followed him.
那个男人走得很快。他一直在看表。
“他一定是要去见什么人。” 鲍比说。男孩们藏在汽车的后面观察着那个男人。他在一辆深色的车前面停了下来。车窗打开了，另一个扎着马尾的男人坐在车里。
The man walked quickly. He kept looking at his watch.
"He must be meeting someone," Bobby said.
Hiding behind a car, the boys watched the man. He stopped in front of a dark van. The window opened. Another man with a ponytail was inside.
鲍比指着那边叫道：“他要上车了！” 
“车号是多少？” 赛伊在找车号。
车子启动了，离开了这条街道。
“我记下了车牌号。” 赛伊说，“我们去找警察吧。” 
男孩儿们跑了又跑，终于找到了一位警官。
Bobby pointed and exclaimed, "He's getting into that van!"
"What's the license plate?" Cy searched for the numbers.
The van started its engine and sped down the street.
"I memorized the numbers," Cy said. "Let's go find the police."
The boys ran and ran until they found an officer.
“警察叔叔！” 他们大叫道。
“哪里着火了？” 警察笑道。
赛伊和鲍比把他们看到的说了一遍。警察不再笑了。他看上去非常严肃，并写下了车牌号。
“谢谢你们的帮助。” 警察说。
"Officer!" they shouted.
"Where's the fire, boys?" the policeman laughed.
Cy and Bobby explained what they'd seen. The officer stopped laughing. He looked very serious as he wrote down the numbers.
"Thanks for your help," the officer said.
然后，男孩儿们回家了。
“怎么这么久？” 玛莎阿姨说着从赛伊手里接过了面包。
男孩儿们解释了刚刚发生的事情。镇上的人们也听到了。不久之后，潘瑟领着一位警察走进了厨房。那就是之前和男孩儿们交谈过的那位警察。
Then the boys headed back home.
"What took so long?" Aunt Martha said as she took the bread from Cy.
The boys explained what had happened. The townspeople listened too. Not long after, Panther walked into the kitchen with a police officer. It was the same one the boys had spoken to.
“我们已经抓住了银行强盗。” 他宣布。
大家都欢呼起来。
“你们要谢谢这些男孩子们。” 警察说着拍了拍男孩们的背。
赛伊和鲍比脸红了。他们的邻居和男孩儿们又是握手又是拥抱。
"The bank robbers have been caught," he announced.
Everyone cheered loudly.
"You can thank the boys," the officer said, patting them on the back.
Cy and Bobby turned red. Their neighbors shook their hands. They gave them hugs.
“我太为你们自豪了。” 玛莎阿姨哭了。
大家都离开后，孩子们一屁股坐到沙发上。外面开始天黑了。
“我觉得这里的人并没有那么坏。” 潘瑟说。
"I'm so proud of you," cried Aunt Martha.
When everyone had left, the children flopped onto the sofa. Outside it was getting dark.
"I guess people here aren't that bad," said Panther.
“但日落之后，他们就会忘记发生过的事情。” 鲍比悲伤地说。
“他们会忘记关于我们的一切。” 赛伊补充说。
“至少我们知道这里的人很友善的。” 简说。
"But after the sun sets, they'll forget what happened," Bobby said in a sad voice.
"They'll forget all about us," Cy added.
"At least we know people can be nice," Jane said.
孩子们看着太阳落山了。一阵急促的敲门声吓了他们一跳。赛伊打开了门，是农夫詹金斯。
“我拿来了一些自家做的果酱给我们的英雄。” 他微笑着说。
“啊？” 鲍比一脸困惑。
The children watched as the sun disappeared. A sharp knock on the door startled them. Cy opened the door. It was Farmer Jenkins.
"I brought some homemade jam for our heroes," he said, smiling.
"Huh?" Bobby was confused.
“为什么？” 潘瑟小心地问。她还记得他们欠农夫詹金斯多少果酱。
“因为你们帮忙抓住了强盗。” 詹金斯轻声笑着说，“我希望你们喜欢这些果酱。” 
"What for?" asked Panther cautiously. She still remembered how much jam they owed Farmer Jenkins.
"For helping to catch the robbers," the farmer chuckled. "I hope you kids enjoy this."
他把果酱罐递给赛伊后就走了。孩子们有些羞愧地看着那些果酱。
“他为什么会记得抢劫的事情？” 鲍比依然觉得困惑。他看着外面漆黑的天空问。“休伊说太阳落山之后，一切都会回到原样的。” 
He handed the jars to Cy then waved good-bye. The children looked at the jam guiltily.
"Why did he remember the robbery?" Bobby was still confused, looking outside at the dark. "Huey said everything returns to normal once the sun sets."
没人可以解释这件事情。
第二天一早，孩子们就去见休伊了。赛伊刚把休伊从井里拉上来，鲍比就抛出了一连串的问题。“哇哈！” 休伊抬起了手，“你再说一次，你的愿望是什么？” 
No one could explain it.
Early the next morning, the children went to see Huey. As soon as Cy pulled him up, Bobby started to throw out questions.
"Whoa!" Huey held up his hand. "What exactly was your wish again?"
“我希望发生一些好玩儿的事情。” 鲍比回答说。
“然后呢，事情发生了吗？” 休伊问。
“昨天发生了一场银行抢劫。” 鲍比说，“那很可怕，不好玩儿。” 
“还发生了其它的事情吗？” 休伊问。
"For something exciting to happen," answered Bobby.
"And did it?" Huey asked.
"There was a bank robbery," said Bobby. "It was more kind of scary than exciting."
"What else happened?" Huey asked.
“我们见到了很多以前不认识的人。” 赛伊说。
“也许银行抢劫并不是你们的愿望。” 休伊说。
“我不明白。” 鲍比说。
"We got to meet many new people," said Cy.
"So maybe the bank robbery wasn't the wish," said Huey.
"I don't understand," said Bobby.
“认识新的人感觉怎么样？” 休伊问。
孩子们陷入了思考。最后鲍比回答。“有儿点意思。” 他说。
"How did it feel to meet new people?" asked Huey.
The children were thoughtful. Finally Bobby answered. "It was kind of exciting," he said.
“也许这才是你的愿望。” 休伊像魔术师一样张开了双手。
“但这个愿望昨天就结束了。” 简说。
“他们现在就不是我们的朋友了吗？” 
“那要看你们的了。” 休伊说。
"Maybe that was your wish." Huey spread his hands like a magician.
"But the wish ended yesterday," said Jane. "Will they stop being our friends now?"
"That depends on you," said Huey.
孩子们围在井边，一言不发。他们已经同意了各自许两个愿望。现在只有潘瑟剩下一个愿望可以许了。
“你们要在这里站上一整天吗？” 休伊说。他的声音听上去有点儿不高兴了。
赛伊用胳膊肘推了推潘瑟。“轮到你了。” 他说。
The children stood around Huey's well without speaking. They had agreed to make two wishes each. Only Panther had one wish left.
"Are you going to stand there all day?" Huey said, sounding irritated.
Cy nudged Panther. "It's your turn," he said.
潘瑟点点头，但却没有说话。
“你们的愿望都要用完了吗？” 休伊问。
潘瑟依然没有说话。
鲍比大声叹了一口气。
简轻轻地用脚踢了一下。赛伊转了转脑袋。他们显然都有点儿烦了，但没人说话。
Panther nodded but didn't say anything.
"Are you running out of wishes?" Huey asked.
Panther still did not speak.
Bobby let out a loud sigh. Jane tapped her foot. Cy kept turning his head. Clearly they were all annoyed. But no one said a word.
“我希望每个人都可以说出他心里想的话。” 潘瑟突然不假思索地说。
休伊的鳍开始一开一合。
潘瑟用手捂住了嘴。
“不，休伊。那不是一个愿望。” 她叫道。
“但你许愿了啊！” 休伊说。他的鳍变成了紫色和蓝色。
"I wish everyone would say what they are thinking," Panther blurted out.
Huey's fins started to open and close. Panther put her hand over her mouth.
"No, Huey. That wasn't a wish," she cried.
"But you wished it," Huey said, his fins turning purple and blue.
“我的意思是……” 潘瑟试图解释，
但已经太迟了。休伊实现了她的愿望。
“现在离开这里吧。” 他说。
赛伊将休伊放到了木桶里。当他慢慢把休伊送回井底时，赛伊轻声说：“我真的厌倦了每次都做这件事。” 
"What I meant was . . ." Panther tried to explain.
But it was too late. Huey granted her wish.
"Now go away," he said.
Cy placed Huey in the bucket. As he lowered him down, Cy muttered, "I'm sick of doing this every time."
他的兄弟姐妹们盯着他。赛伊猛地用手捂住了嘴。“我为什么说出来了？” 他惊奇地说。
“那是因为你很刻薄。” 简喊道。她随后也用手捂住了嘴。
孩子们突然感觉面对彼此很尴尬。
His siblings stared at him. Cy slapped a hand over his mouth. "Why did I say that?" he said in a surprised voice.
"That's because you're mean," Jane cried out. She, too, then covered her mouth.
The children suddenly felt uneasy with each other.
“我觉得我们应该回家了。” 潘瑟建议说。
孩子们走出了森林。
潘瑟领头走着。她有很多事情需要思考。突然，赛伊从后面叫住了她：“你能不能慢一点？” 
"I think we should go home," Panther suggested.
The children headed out of the woods. Panther walked ahead of the others. She had much to think about. Suddenly, Cy called out from behind, "Will you slow down?"
潘瑟转头嚷道：“你干吗不走快点？你真慢！"
“真慢？你说谁慢？” 赛伊叫嚷着，“你总是觉得你比我们都强！” “对，我本来就是！” 潘瑟叫嚷着回击。
Panther spun her head around and shouted: "Why don't you walk faster? You're such a slowpoke!"
"Slowpoke? Who are you calling slow?" yelled Cy. "You always think you're better than us!"
"Well, I am!" Panther shouted back.
“你俩都住嘴！” 鲍比爆发了。
潘瑟和赛伊都吃惊地盯着他们的弟弟。
“你让我住嘴？” 赛伊生气地问道。
“对！你俩就像两只猴子！” 鲍比叫着。
“你看上去才像只猴子呢！” 简对着鲍比大嚷。
"Shut up, both of you!" exploded Bobby.
Stunned, Panther and Cy stared at their younger brother.
"Did you tell me to shut up?" Cy said in a low, angry voice.
"Yes! You're acting like two baboons!" Bobby shouted.
"You're the one who looks like a monkey," Jane yelled at Bobby.
很快四个孩子都相互叫骂了起来。
“所以这才是你们对我真正的看法。” 潘瑟哭了。
“我还以为你们关心我。” 鲍比大喊。
过了一会儿，孩子们都没话要说了。
“这是最糟糕的愿望。” 简抽着鼻子说。
Soon all four children were shouting and screaming at each other.
"So this is how you really feel about me," wept Panther.
"I thought you cared about me," cried Bobby.
After a while, the children ran out of things to say.
"This is the worst wish ever," sniffed Jane.
潘瑟一句话都没说，因为在她的心里也是同意的。孩子们相互不再说话，都沉默着继续往家走。当他们接近镇子的时候，孩子们听到了到处都在尖叫。
“我讨厌你！” 基弗先生对着一个年轻女人嚷嚷着。
“好，我也从来没有喜欢过你！” 那个女人反击道。
Panther didn't say anything because in her heart she agreed. Without speaking to one another, they all continued to walk. As they got closer to town, the children heard shouting everywhere.
"I don't like you!" shouted Mr. Keefer to a young woman.
"Well, I never liked you either!" she shouted back.
然后，孩子们看到了带着兰姆的玛莎阿姨。她在和农夫詹金斯的妻子说话。
“你的发型太丑了！” 詹金斯夫人说。
“什么？” 玛莎阿姨摸了摸头发。“好吧， 你的裙子看上去就像渔夫！” 她说。
Then the children spotted Aunt Martha with Lamb. She was speaking with Farmer Jenkins' wife.
"Your hairstyle is ugly!" Mrs. Jenkins said.
"What?" Aunt Martha touched her hair. "Well, you dress like a fisherman!" she said.
“哦，是吗？那总比你穿的像个老太太好！” 詹金斯夫人大叫。
这两个女人看上去就要打起来了。潘瑟向他们跑过去。“你们不是那个意思的。” 她恳求地说，“你们两个是朋友啊。” 
"Oh, really? It's better than dressing like an old lady!" Mrs. Jenkins shouted.
The two women looked as if they were going to wrestle. Panther ran up to them. "You don't mean that," she said in a pleading voice. "You two are friends."
“她才不是我的朋友！” 玛莎阿姨大声说。
“她绝对不是我朋友！” 詹金斯夫人叫嚷着回击。
潘瑟的眼里充满了泪水。
“哦，我都干了些什么？” 她哀叹道。
"She's no friend of mine!" yelled Aunt Martha.
"She's certainly not my friend!" Mrs. Jenkins shot back.
Panther's eyes filled with tears.
"Oh, what have I done?" she moaned.
这情景糟透了。到处都有人在吵架打架。有些人甚至在大哭。玛莎阿姨和詹金斯夫人看都不看对方一眼。
“你们没有什么好的话要说吗？” 潘瑟问。
两个女人都固执地摇摇头。
It was not a pleasant sight. Everywhere people were arguing and fighting. Some were even crying. Aunt Martha and Mrs. Jenkins would not look at each other.
"Don't you have anything nice to say to each other?" Panther asked.
Both women stubbornly shook their heads.
鲍比和赛伊陷入了另一场争论里。
“你的行为就像个小孩子。” 赛伊说。
“你太喜欢发号施令了。” 鲍比回击说。
与此同时，简在和基弗先生说话。“我最欢的糖果是红樱桃的。” 她说。
Bobby and Cy got into another argument.
"You act like a baby," Cy said.
"You're too bossy," Bobby said in return.
Meanwhile, Jane was speaking with Mr. Keefer. "My favorite candy is red cherry," she said.
“我觉得你吃太多糖了。” 店主基弗先生说。
“我吃的糖没有鲍比多。” 她大声回答，“所以他才这么胖。” 
潘瑟忍不下去了。
“就没人能说点儿好听的话吗？” 她大叫。
"I think you eat too many sweets," the shop owner said.
"I don't eat as much as Bobby," she loudly replied. "That's why he's so fat."
Panther could not take it anymore!
"Doesn't anyone have a nice word to say?" she shouted.
有那么一秒钟，人们都停止了说话。他们都瞪着潘瑟。
“我的天，你的声音真的是太大了！” 詹金斯夫人讨厌地说。
“你真的非常粗鲁。” 一个男人皱着眉说。
潘瑟真想钻到地缝里去。一切都是她的错。
“潘特。” 兰姆轻轻地叫着。
For a second, people stopped talking. They all stared at Panther.
"My, your voice certainly is loud," Mrs. Jenkins said nastily.
"You're very rude," said a man, frowning.
Panther wanted to crawl under a rock. Everything was her fault.
"Pan-ter," Lamb cooed.
他伸出了胖胖的小胳膊。潘瑟把他从玛莎阿姨那里抱了过来。兰姆咯咯笑着。潘瑟看着兰姆的眼睛。她看了很久。
“你没有什么不好的，兰姆。” 她温柔地说。她看看四周，补充说：“我希望我们都能像你一样。” 
He reached out his chubby arms. Panther took him from Aunt Martha. Lamb giggled and smiled. Panther looked into his eyes for a long time.
"There's nothing bad about you, Lamb," she said softly. Looking around, she added, "I wish we could all be like you."
潘瑟仔细想了想她刚才说的话。“你只想着人们好的地方。” 她告诉兰姆。
这让她有了一个主意。
“玛莎阿姨。” 她突然说。
“怎么了，孩子？” 玛莎阿姨尖声问道。
Panther thought about what she had just said. "You only think good things about people," she said to Lamb.
That gave her an idea.
"Aunt Martha," she said suddenly.
"What is it, child?" Aunt Martha asked sharply.
“谢谢您对我们这么好。” 潘瑟说。
她认真的思考了自己真正的感受。“我们知道照顾我们很不容易。” 她继续说，“所以我们很珍惜。” 
“哦，你们这些孩子有时很烦人。” 玛莎阿姨真心地说，“但至少你们很懂礼貌。” 
"Thank you for being so kind to us," Panther said.
She thought about how she really felt. "We know it's not easy to look after us," she continued. "So we really appreciate it."
"Well, you children can be annoying," Aunt Martha said truthfully. "But at least you have good manners."
潘瑟走向了赛伊和鲍比。“你们就没有什么好话能说吗？” 她问自己的兄弟。鲍比固执地抱着胳膊摇头。赛伊将脑袋转到一边，对鲍比视而不见。
“看看你们自己的内心。” 潘瑟对自己的兄弟们说，“我肯定你们会找到好的一面，而不仅仅是不好的一面。” 
Panther went over to Cy and Bobby. "Don't you have anything nice to say?" she asked her brothers. Bobby crossed his arms stubbornly and shook his head. Cy turned his head and ignored him.
"Look into your hearts," she said to her siblings. "I'm sure you'll find good things, not just bad."
孩子们看看彼此。鲍比对着赛伊吐了吐舌头。
潘瑟大声叹了口气，然后说：“人们应该说出自己内心的想法。但如果我们心里想的只有美好的事情呢？” 
镇上的一些居民停止了争吵去听潘瑟说话。
“真心表达自己并不意味着要成为被人讨厌的人。” 她继续说。
The children looked at each other. Bobby stuck his tongue out at Cy.
Panther sighed loudly and said: "People should say what they think. But what if we thought only nice things?"
Some of the townspeople stopped to listen to Panther.
"Being honest doesn't mean being nasty," she continued.
鲍比看了看赛伊。赛伊迅速对他一笑。
“有时候，语言并不是必须的。” 潘瑟说，“但如果你必须说点什么，你可以说一些好听的。” 
“好吧，虽然赛伊欺负我，” 鲍比说，“但有时候他很酷。” 
Bobby glanced at Cy. Cy gave him a quick smile.
"And sometimes, words may not even be necessary," Panther said. "But if you have to say something, then try to say something kind."
"Well, Cy is a big bully," said Bobby. "But sometimes he's cool."
“虽然鲍比是个胖子，” 简咯咯笑道，“但他很有意思。” 
鲍比脸红了。“我不胖！” 他嚷到。
赛伊很快打断了他弟弟的话。
“潘瑟说得对。” 他说，“我们可以用友好的方式做到诚实。对吧，简？” 
"Even though Bobby is fat," giggled Jane, "he's funny."
Bobby's face turned red. "I'm not fat!" he yelled.
Cy quickly interrupted his siblings.
"Panther is right," he said. "We can be truthful in a nice way. Right, Jane?"
简依然在咯咯笑，但她点了点头。兰姆给了潘瑟一个大大的拥抱。她紧紧地抱住了兰姆。然后，玛莎阿姨从潘瑟那里把兰姆接了过去。“好了，孩子们！” 她说，“我们回家吧。” 
Jane was still giggling but she nodded. Lamb gave Panther a big hug. She held him tightly. Then Aunt Martha took Lamb from Panther.
"All right, children," she said, "let's get home."
她从詹金斯夫人身边走过的时候，她停下来笑了。詹金斯夫人也笑了。其他人也都停止了争吵。有的人又重新有了笑容。
As she passed Mrs. Jenkins, she paused and smiled. Mrs. Jenkins smiled back. Other people also stopped arguing. Some even started to laugh again.
当他们到家的时候，孩子们坐在了外面的门廊里。太阳依然在天上闪闪发光。
“你们觉得这个愿望怎样才能结束？” 鲍比问。
“就像潘瑟许愿的那样啊。” 赛伊说，“我们会说出我们心里想的话。” 
“但我们只能想好的想法啊。” 简说。
When they got home, the children sat outside on the porch. The sun was still shining brightly.
"How do you think this wish will end?" asked Bobby.
"Just as Panther wished," said Cy. "We'll speak what's on our mind."
"But we'll be thinking only nice thoughts," said Jane.
“至少我们会这样去努力。” 鲍比说。他依然为简说他胖生着气。
巴斯家的孩子们紧紧地坐在一起。
“有你们在我身边真是太幸运了。” 潘瑟对她的兄弟姐妹说，“这才是我的心里话。” 
"At least we'll try to," said Bobby. He was still upset that Jane had called him fat.
The Barth children sat close together.
"I'm so lucky to have you," Panther said to her siblings. "And that's truly from my heart."
第二天早上，巴斯家的孩子们感觉好多了，但他们依然非常想念自己的父母。他们的爸爸妈妈已经离开一周了。当然，他们每晚都会打电话过来，但这跟爸爸妈妈真的在身边还是不一样的。
The next morning, the Barth children were feeling much better. Still, they missed their parents very much. It was over a week since Mom and Dad had left. Of course they called every night. But it wasn't the same.
“我希望妈妈和爸爸能快点儿回来。” 鲍比说。
“我太想念他们了。” 简说。她都快哭了。
“爸爸得先找到工作。” 赛伊勇敢地说。
实际上，在他心里，他也很想念爸爸妈妈。
“我希望他今天就能找到工作。” 潘瑟说。
"I hope Mom and Dad come home soon," said Bobby.
"I miss them so much," Jane said, almost in tears.
"Dad has to find a job first," said Cy bravely. Inside, he, too, missed his parents.
"I wish he would find one today," said Panther.
这几个孩子几乎是同时抬起了眼睛。
“你们想的是不是和我一样？” 赛伊问。
“休伊。” 鲍比小声说。
“但我们不能再许愿了。” 潘瑟分辨说，“我们每个人都许过两个愿了。结束了！” 
Almost at the same time, the children looked up.
"Are you thinking what I'm thinking?" asked Cy.
"Huey," Bobby said under his breath.
"But we said no more wishes," argued Panther. "Each of us got two wishes. That's it!"
“但这次不一样。” 赛伊说，“这不是为了我们。这是为了妈妈和爸爸。” 
孩子们思考着。“而且我们还没有向休伊告别呢。” 简补充道。
“你说的对。” 潘瑟承认了。
"But this is different," said Cy. "It's not for us. It's for Mom and Dad."
The children thought about this.
"And we didn't say good-bye to Huey," added Jane.
"That's true," admitted Panther.
于是，孩子们又一次出发去见休伊了。赛伊和往常一样把木桶降到了井底，但他用了更长时间才找到休伊的壳。最终，赛伊找到了休伊并把他拉了上来。
“你躲在哪儿了，休伊？” 这个神奇生物出现的时候赛伊问。
So the children set out to see Huey once again. Cy lowered the bucket as usual. But it took longer to locate Huey's shell. Finally Cy found him and pulled him up.
"Where were you, Huey?" asked Cy when the magical creature appeared.
“就是忙一些事情。” 休伊回答。他避开了孩子们的眼睛。
休伊看上去和他以前的样子有点儿不同了，但孩子们不敢问他遇到了什么事情。
“今天轮到谁了？” 休伊说。他恢复了平常的声音。
"Just busy with something," Huey answered, avoiding their eyes.
Huey seemed different from his usual self. But the children were afraid to ask if something had happened to him.
"Whose turn is it today?" Huey said, his voice back to normal.
孩子们看看彼此，然后赛伊回答说：“哦，这是我们大家的愿望。” 他指着自己的兄弟姐妹还有自己。
休伊看上去很吃惊：“这很少见啊。那你们的愿望是什么呢？” 
“我们希望爸爸能找到工作。” 赛伊说。
The children looked at each other. Then Cy answered, "Well, it's our wish." He pointed to his siblings and himself.
Huey looked surprised. "That's unusual. What is your wish, then?"
"We wish our dad would find a job," said Cy.
“然后快点回家。” 简补充说。
“但是，你们知道规矩的。” 休伊说。
他盯着孩子们发愣的脸：“愿望的有效期只有一天，还记得吗？” 他提醒他们说。
“哦，是啊。” 他们伤心地回答。
"And hurry and come home," added Jane.
"But you know the rules," said Huey.
He stared at the children's blank faces. "The wish is granted for only one day, remember?" he reminded them.
"Oh, yeah," they replied sadly.
“但这真的是我们最最想实现的愿望啊。” 鲍比恳求说，“其它愿望都是为了好玩儿。这个是真的愿望。” 
“对啊，这个是真正的愿望。” 简说。
休伊慢慢地摇摇头：“我很抱歉，孩子们，但规矩就是规矩。” 
"But this is the only wish we really, really want," pleaded Bobby. "The other wishes were for fun. This is for real."
"Yeah, this is a real wish," said Jane.
Huey shook his head slowly. "I'm sorry, children. But rules are rules."
休伊的鳍开始闪烁光芒。这次的颜色比以往都要多，但孩子们太伤心了，都没有注意到。
“好了，休伊。我们明白了。” 最后，赛伊说。
“那我们就没有别的愿望了。” 潘瑟说。
赛伊轻轻地将休伊放回了木桶里。
Huey's fins started to glow and shine. The colors were more beautiful than ever. But the children were too sad to notice.
"Okay, Huey. We understand," Cy said finally.
"Then we don't have any more wishes," said Panther.
Cy gently placed Huey back into the bucket.
“开心点吧。” 休伊说，“我敢肯定，你们的爸爸妈妈很快就回家了。” 
说完这句话，休伊就消失在了他的壳里。赛伊再一次将他送回了井底。
孩子们带着沉重的心走回了家，脸色也很难看。
"Cheer up," said Huey. "I'm sure your parents will be home soon."
With that he disappeared into his shell. Cy lowered him down once again.
With heavy hearts, the children walked back home. They spent the rest of the day with long faces.
“我知道什么能让咱们高兴起来。” 潘瑟说。
孩子们坐在门廊里吃着零食。“我们应该为每个人烤点饼干。” 
“为什么？” 鲍比问。他的嘴里塞满了花生酱。
"I know what will cheer us up," Panther said. The children were sitting on the porch eating a snack. "We should bake cookies for everyone."
"What for?" asked Bobby, his mouth full of peanut butter.
“嗯，在遇到休伊之后，发生了这么多事情。” 潘瑟继续说，“我们的愿望影响了其他人。” 
“玛莎阿姨、贝克尔先生和西尔弗。” 简数着数，“还有农夫詹金斯。” 
“而且我们交了很多朋友。” 潘瑟说。
"Well, after meeting Huey, so many things happened," Panther continued. "Our wishes affected other people."
"Aunt Martha, Mr. Becker and Silver," Jane counted, "and Farmer Jenkins."
"And we made a lot of friends," said Panther.
“这想法不错。” 赛伊开心地说。
于是，孩子们烤了很多饼干。当然，玛莎阿姨和兰姆也帮了忙。屋外天色开始变暗了。
“我们可以明天拿这些饼干去送给每个人。” 潘瑟说。
突然，响起了敲门声。
"That's a great idea," Cy said happily.
So the children baked lots of cookies. Of course Aunt Martha and Lamb helped too. Outside it was getting dark.
"We can take the cookies to everyone tomorrow," said Panther.
Suddenly there was a knock on the door.
“我去开门。” 简说。
“我在想会是谁呢？” 鲍比一边问，一边又从盘子里拿了一块饼干。
“惊喜哦！” 两个熟悉的声音大声叫道。那是妈妈和爸爸，他们回来了。
"I'll get it," said Jane.
"I wonder who it can be?" asked Bobby as he took another cookie from a plate.
"Surprise!" two familiar voices shouted loudly.
Mom and Dad had returned.
对巴斯一家人来说，这一切都发生的太快了。爸爸在城里找到了一份很好的工作，所以他们马上就要搬回去了。
孩子们第二天很早就起床了。他们带着自家做的饼干，拜访了邻居们。他们希望说声谢谢，也想说声再见。
Everything happened quickly for the Barth family. Dad had found a good job in the city. So they had to move back immediately.
The children got up early the next morning. They visited their neighbors with the homemade cookies. They wanted to say thank you. And they wanted to say good-bye.
“这些饼干是为您准备的。” 孩子们对詹金斯一家人说。
然后，赛伊和简拜访了基弗先生。潘瑟和鲍比去看了贝克尔先生和西尔弗。他们很舍不得这些新朋友。
"These cookies are for you," the children said to the Jenkins.
Then Cy and Jane visited Mr. Keefer. Panther and Bobby went to see Mr. Becker and Silver. It was not easy to say good-bye to their new friends.
他们回到家里的时候，爸爸已经装好车了。
“快点儿打包吧，孩子们。” 他叫着孩子们，“我们没有太多时间了。” 
孩子们盯着车旁边的一个个箱子。突然，潘瑟深吸了一口气。“我们还没有跟休伊说再见！” 她低声说。
When they got home, Dad was already loading the car.
"Hurry and pack, kids," he called to them. "We don't have a lot of time."
The children stared at the boxes beside the car. Suddenly Panther gasped. "We didn't say good-bye to Huey!" she whispered.
孩子们朝着树林跑去。
“你们要去哪里啊？” 爸爸叫道。
“我们去跟一个朋友道别！” 赛伊大声回答。
孩子们并没有停下。他们一直跑到了休伊的井边。赛伊赶快将木桶放了下去。
The children began running toward the woods.
"Where are you going?" Dad shouted.
"To say good-bye to a friend!" Cy yelled back.
The children did not stop until they got to Huey's well. Cy hurriedly lowered the bucket down.
他又一次费了很大功夫才找到休伊的壳。最后，他还是把休伊拉了上来。就像昨天一样，休伊看上去有点儿不同了。他甚至对着孩子们微笑了。
“你们是来许最后一个愿望的吗？” 他说。
“最后一个愿望？你要去哪里吗？” 潘瑟问。
Once again, he struggled to find Huey's shell. Finally he pulled Huey up. As he had the day before, Huey seemed different. He even smiled at the children.
"Did you come to make your last wish?" he said.
"Last wish? Are you going somewhere?" asked Panther.
“我以为你们要走了。” 休伊说。
“你怎么知道的？” 鲍比问。
休伊再次微笑着说：“我们奥伦族知道我们需要知道的一切。” 
"I thought you were leaving," Huey said.
"How did you know?" asked Bobby.
Huey smiled again and said, "We Orlanes know what we need to know."
“休伊。” 潘瑟说。她的声音开始哽咽了：“我们会想你的。” 
其他人也伤心地点头。
“谢谢你实现了我们的愿望。” 赛伊感激地说。
“我们很抱歉，但我们没有什么能给你的。” 简哭了。
"Huey," Panther said, her voice choking, "we're going to miss you."
The others nodded sadly.
"Thank you for making our wishes come true," Cy said gratefully.
"We're sorry but we don't have anything to give you," Jane cried.
“我们烤了一些饼干。” 鲍比说，“但我们把它们都送出去了。” 
“饼干？” 休伊做了个鬼脸，“我很高兴你们没有把饼干给我。我不喜欢人类的食物。” 然后，他更温柔地看着每个孩子：“但谢谢你们想到我。” 
"We baked some cookies," said Bobby. "But we gave them all away."
"Cookies?" Huey made a face. "I'm glad you didn't give them to me. I don't like human food." Then he looked at each child more gently. "But thank you for thinking of me."
“我们还能来看你吗？” 简满怀希望地问。
“恐怕不行了。” 休伊说着摇摇头，“因为，我也要离开了。” 
“离开？” 孩子们大喊，“去哪里啊？” 
“去我的下一站。” 休伊回答。
"Can we come to visit you sometime?" Jane asked hopefully.
"I'm afraid not," said Huey, shaking his head. "For I, too, am leaving."
"Leaving?" cried the children. "Where?"
"To my next destination," Huey replied.
“那我们怎么才能找到你呢？” 简哭了。她完全不在意眼泪从脸上滚落。
休伊的鳍开始一开一合。他的脑袋从一边转到另一边。然后，他的整个身体都开始发光，很亮很亮。
"How can we find you?" Jane cried, not caring that tears were falling down her face.
Huey's fins began to open and close. His head moved from side to side. Then his whole body glowed brightly.
“总有一天。” 他说。“你们会遇到另一口井的。你们朝里看，也许我就在那里。但是，” 他继续说道，“在你们的心灵更深处去寻找。你们就一定会找到我的。” 
“我们会想你的，休伊。” 孩子们流着泪说。
"One day," he said, "you may come across another well. Look inside and perhaps I may be there. But," he continued, "search deeper in your hearts. And you will find me for sure."
"We're going to miss you, Huey," the children said, weeping.
他们站在那里哭着，没人说话。最后，赛伊开口了。
“我想我们必须得走了。” 他抽着鼻子说。
“不过别忘了把我放回井里去。” 休伊说。
赛伊充满爱地捧起了休伊的壳，把它轻轻地放到了木桶里。
They stood there in tears for a few minutes. Nobody said a word. Finally, Cy spoke up.
"I think we have to go," he said, sniffling.
"Just don't forget to lower me back down," Huey said.
Cy lovingly lifted Huey's shell. He gently placed it inside the bucket.
“再见，赛伊，潘瑟，鲍比，还有简！” 休伊挥着手说。
“再见，休伊！” 孩子们大叫道。
赛伊最后一次将木桶放回了井里。孩子们看着休伊的壳消失在了那个黑黑的洞里。
就在孩子们转身准备回家的时候，潘瑟说：“我还有最后一个愿望。” 
"Good-bye, Cy! Panther! Bobby and Jane!" Huey waved.
"Good-bye, Huey!" cried the children.
Then for the last time Cy lowered the bucket down the well. The children watched as Huey's shell disappeared down the dark hole.
"I have one last wish," said Panther, as the children turned to go home.
“但休伊已经走了。” 赛伊说。
“没关系的。” 她回答。
“你的愿望是什么？” 简问。
“我希望休伊一切都好。我希望他去任何地方都能找到快乐。” 潘瑟说。
"But Huey's gone," said Cy.
"It doesn't matter," she answered.
"What is it?" asked Jane.
"I wish the best for Huey. I wish he finds happiness wherever he goes," Panther said.
“你怎么知道这个愿望会成真呢？” 简问。
“因为生命中最好的愿望就是你为他人许下的愿望。” 潘瑟回答说。
巴斯家的孩子们手牵着手走出了森林。
"How do you know it will come true?" asked Jane.
"Because the best wishes in life are those you make for others," replied Panther.
Hand in hand, the Barth children walked out of the woods.
